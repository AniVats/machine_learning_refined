Kempner Professor &amp; Chair of <a href=\"https://www.cs.yale.edu\">Computer Science</a>, <a href=\"https://www.yale.edu\">Yale University</a></h4>\n\n<p>Welcome to my home page! I am interested in doing research on\nprogramming languages, formal methods, operating systems, and computer\nsecurity. I am an advocate of <a href=\"https://cacm.acm.org/magazines/2010/12/102141-certified-software/fulltext\">certified software</a>. I believe that certified programming with mechanized\nproofs is the most promising approach toward\nbuilding truly dependable software and taking control of the rapidly\ngrowing complexity in future computer systems.</p>\n\n<p>\n<a href=\"https://flint.cs.yale.edu/certikos\"><img id=\"certikoslogo\" src=\"https://flint.cs.yale.edu/flint/images/certikos.png\" alt=\"\"></a> I\nlead the <strong><a href=\"https://flint.cs.yale.edu\">FLINT</a></strong> group at Yale. We are interested in\nbuilding novel <a href=\"https://flint.cs.yale.edu/certikos\">certified\nsystem software</a>.  Our work spans many fields, ranging from\nprogramming language design, realistic OS kernel hacking, formal\nsemantics and logics, compiler development, and proof engineering, to\nsolving difficult problems related to all aspects of concurrency and\ndistributed computing.\n</p>\n\n<p>\n<a href=\"https://deepspec.org\"><img id=\"dslogo\" src=\"https://deepspec.org/img/deepspec-logo-300dpi.png\" alt=\"\"></a>\nI am also working with researchers at Princeton, U. Penn, and MIT\non the new NSF Expedition project:   \n<a href=\"https://deepspec.org\">The Science of Deep Specification</a>.\n</p>\n\n<p>\nCheck out our recent POPL'15 paper\non <a href=\"https://flint.cs.yale.edu/publications/dscal.html\">Deep\n    Specifications and Certified Abstraction Layers</a>\nand PLDI'18 paper on\n<a href=\"https://flint.cs.yale.edu/publications/ccal.html\">\n    Certified Concurrent Abstraction Layers</a> for an overview of our\nnew layered approach for building certified software.  See\nour <a href=\"https://flint.cs.yale.edu/publications/certikos.html\">new\nOSDI'16 paper</a> (and the\nofficial <a href=\"https://news.yale.edu/2016/11/14/certikos-breakthrough-toward-hacker-resistant-operating-systems\">Yale\npress release</a>) for an overview of our latest breakthrough on\nbuilding certified hacker-resistant concurrent operating systems.</p>\n\n<p>\nI am looking for <a href=\"jobs.html\">new PostDocs and \nPhD students</a>.\n&nbsp; \nFeel free to contact me if you\nhave interests in or around my research areas, are creative and\nhighly motivated, and have strong technical skills.\n</p>\n\n<p>\nI am teaching\n<a href=\"https://flint.cs.yale.edu/cs428\">CS428/528 Language-Based Security</a> in Spring 2020.
