Below is a description of my recent projects.\n\n<!-- <br><br><b>I am looking for Ph.D. students and postdocs interested in the theory and practice of parallel algorithms, data structures, and programming.</b> -->\n\n\n<h3>Large-scale Graph Processing</h3> I am very interested in\ndeveloping algorithms for large-scale graph processing. Graph\nalgorithms have many applications, ranging from analyzing social\nnetworks to finding patterns in biological networks.\n\nI have developed <a href=\"http://jshun.github.io/ligra/\" target=\"_blank\">Ligra</a>, a lightweight graph\nprocessing framework for shared memory.  The project was motivated by\nthe fact that the largest publicly available real-world graphs all fit\nin shared memory.  When graphs fit in shared memory, processing them\nusing Ligra can give performance improvements of up to orders of\nmagnitude compared to distributed-memory graph processing systems.  I\nhave also developed Ligra+, an extension of Ligra that uses graph\ncompression techniques to process large graphs with less\nmemory. Recently, I have used Ligra/Ligra+ to design and evaluate\nparallel algorithms for graph eccentricity estimation as well as local graph clustering.\n\n<ul>\n\n    <li> Julian Shun\n<br><a
