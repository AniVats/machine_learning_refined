Professor Akinwande received a\n      B.Sc. (1978) in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from the University\n      of Ife, Nigeria, a MS (1981) and Ph.D. (1986) in Electrical Engineering\n      from Stanford University, Stanford, California. </p>\n    <p>Professor Akinwande joined Honeywell Inc. in 1986 where he initially conducted\n      research on GaAs Complementary FET technology for very high speed and low\n      power signal processing. He later joined the Si Microstructures group where\n      he conducted research on pressure sensors, accelerometers, thin-film field\n      emission and display devices. </p>\n    <p>Professor Akinwande joined MIT\u2019s Microsystems Technology Laboratories\n      (MTL) in January 1995 where his research focuses on micro-fabrication and\n      electronic devices with particular emphasis on smart sensors and actuators,\n      intelligent displays, large area electronics (macro-electronics), field\n      emission &amp; field ionization devices, mass spectrometry and electric\n      propulsion. </p>\n    <p>Prof. Akinwande is a recipient of the 1996 National Science Foundation\n      (NSF) Career Award. He has served a number of technical program committees\n      for various conferences, including the Device Research Conference, the\n      International Electron Devices Meeting, the International Solid-State Circuits\n      Conference, the International Display Research Conference and the International\n      Vacuum Microelectronics Conference. Professor Akinwande holds numerous\n      patents in MEMS, Electronics on Flexible Substrates, Display technologies\n      and has authored more than 100 journal publications. He was a visiting\n      professor at the Cambridge University Engineering Department and an Overseas\n      Fellow of Churchill College in 2002-2003.&nbsp; He is a current member\n      of the IEEE Nanotechnology Council.</p>\n    <h3>Research Interests </h3>\n    <p>Microstructures and nanostructures for sensors and actuators, and vacuum\n      microelectronics.
