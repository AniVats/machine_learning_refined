Science at MIT, and a member of <a href=\"https://lids.mit.edu\" target=\"_blank\">LIDS</a> and <a href=\"https://stat.mit.edu\" target=\"_blank\">Center for Statistics</a>, which are part of  <a I completed my undergraduate degree in ECE at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign.</p>\n\n<p>My research is broadly at the interface of information theory, statistics, theoretical computer science, and applied probability. I seek to obtain engineering insight into practically relevant problems by formulating and solving mathematical models. As part of this, I want to understand the relationship between combinatorial structure and computational tractability of high-\ndimensional inference in graphical models and other statistical models.</p>\n\n<p>I advise these amazing students and postdoc: <a href=\"http://web.mit.edu/~eboix/www/\" target=\"_blank\">Enric Boix-Adser\u00e0</a>,  <a href=\"http://www.mit.edu/~brennanm/\"
