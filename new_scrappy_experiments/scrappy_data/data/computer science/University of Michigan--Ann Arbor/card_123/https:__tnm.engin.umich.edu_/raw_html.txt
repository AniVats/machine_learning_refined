href=\"http://tnm.engin.umich.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/353/2019/01/1973-MS-Thesis_Semantrix-A-Semantically-Guided-Digital-Electronic-Machine.pdf\">MS</a>&nbsp;and a <a In 2014 he received the<a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eckert\u2013Mauchly_Award\" class=\"external\" target=\"_blank\"> ACM/IEEE CS Eckert-Mauchly Award</a>&nbsp;(<a href=\"http://tnm.engin.umich.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/353/2019/08/Eckert-Mauchly_Talkfinal_June2014.pptx\">slides</a>) and the <a href=\"https://cs.illinois.edu/about-us/awards/alumni-awards\" class=\"external\" target=\"_blank\">University of Illinois Distinguished Alumni Award</a>. His &nbsp;Erd<i>\u0151</i>s number is 3.</p>\n<hr>\n<h4><strong>Computer Architecture and Design</strong></h4>\n<p>Most of my research and teaching focusses on computer architecture and related areas.</p>\n<p><strong>Shown Above</strong> is a 128-core multiprocessor, Centip3De, that was designed and built by our group at Michigan in 2010. It is remarkable in that it stacks seven silicon dies on top of one another. Four of the die contain the 128 (64 each) cores with their caches, two of the die are DRAM memory and the seventh die contains sense amps and spreader to the package pins.</p>\n<h4><strong>The Six Ideas in Computer Architecture&nbsp;</strong></h4>\n<div>Surprisingly, there are only six distinct concepts used to design computers, apart from technology advances, &nbsp;that reappear in many guises&nbsp; over and over again:&nbsp;</div>\n<ol>\n<li>Locality \u2013 spatial &amp; temporal,&nbsp;e.g. caches</li>\n<li>Prediction \u2013 no state change,&nbsp;e.g. branch prediction</li>\n<li>Speculation \u2013 state change,&nbsp;e.g. prefetching</li>\n<li>Indirection,&nbsp;e.g. virtualization</li>\n<li>Parallelism,&nbsp;e.g. pipelining, OoO, vectors</li>\n<li>Specialization,&nbsp;e.g. GPUs, accelerators</li>\n</ol>\n<hr>\n<h3><strong>Teaching</strong></h3>\n<p>Doctor (examining a life insurance prospect): \u201cDo you ever talk in your sleep?\u201d<br>\nProspect: \u201cNo, but I often talk in other people\u2019s sleep\u201d<br>\nDoctor: \u201cBut how can that be?\u201d<br>\nProspect: \u201cI\u2019m a college professor.\u201d<br>\n&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; <em>#230 in \u201cTen Hundred Laughs\u201d Ray Scruggs Houston TX 1928</em></p>\n<hr>\n<p><strong>Quotes</strong></p>\n<p>\u201cThe empires of the future are the empires of the mind.\u201d &nbsp;<strong>Churchill</strong>\u2014good prediction from an unlikely source&nbsp;</p>\n<p>\u201cThings that can\u2019t go on forever, don\u2019t.\u201d attributed to Herb Stein (c. 1985)\u2014the end of Moore\u2019s Law?</p>\n<p>\u201cWe cannot justify our assumptions about the future based on past experience unless there is a law that the future will always resemble the past.
