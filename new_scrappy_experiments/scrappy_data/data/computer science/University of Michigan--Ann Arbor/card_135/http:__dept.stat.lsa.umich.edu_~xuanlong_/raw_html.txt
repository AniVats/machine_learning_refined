I work with richly structured data, such as those \nextracted from texts, images and other \nspatiotemporal signals. \n\n<p> I am particularly interested in a\nfield in statistics known as Bayesian nonparametrics, which provides \na fertile and powerful mathematical framework for the development of \nmany computational and statistical modeling ideas. The spirit of \nBayesian nonparametric statistics is to enable the kind of \ninferential procedures according to which both the statistical modeling \nand computational complexity may adapt to increasingly large and \ncomplex data patterns in a graceful and effective way.\nIn this framework, stochastic processes and random measures, along with \nlatent variable models such as mixture, hierarchical and graphical \nmodels figure prominently. My students and I seek to understand \nthe interaction\nbetween statistical inference and the theory of optimal transport \nthat arises in the learning of complex hierarchical models.\n\n</p><p> My motivation for all this came originally from an interest in\nmachine learning, which continues to be an area of major activities.\nA primary focus in our machine learning research is to develop more effective \ninference algorithms using variational, stochastic and geometric viewpoints.\n\n</p><p><b>Fun statistics:</b> number of papers I have written that has a giant's name\nin the title, as of twenty eighteen: Bayes = 6; Dirichlet = 4; Gauss = 2; Wasserstein = 1; Lyapunov = 1; \nNames I wish to appear on my paper's title: Blackwell, De Finetti, Riemann, Stein, \n\n\n<!--<li> Program Committee's Area Chair: AISTATS (2015, 2017), ICML (2015, 2017, 2018), IJCAI (2016)\n<p>\n<li> Best Paper Award, 21st International Conference on Machine Learning, 2004\n<li> Young Author's Best Paper Award by the IEEE Signal Processing Society, 2007\n<li> Leon O.
