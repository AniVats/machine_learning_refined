Examples include search engines that can deliver the right kind of personalized information at the right time, and intelligent tutoring systems that learn when and how to be most helpful in teaching a particular student.  Building effective, reliable systems like these will require new theoretical, algorithmic, and methodological advances in multiple research areas, including machine learning, optimization, information retrieval, and human-computer interaction. My current research is centered on education, but I'm also interested in mobile and health-related applications.</p>\n\t\t<p>Before joining the University of Michigan in Fall 2013, I was a researcher at <a href=\"https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/\">Microsoft Research</a>, in the <a href=\"http://research.microsoft.com/en-us/groups/clues/\">Context, Learning, and User Experience for Search (CLUES) Group</a>.</p>\n\t\tMy Ph.D. is from the School of Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University, where my advisor was <a href=\"http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~callan\">Jamie Callan</a>\n\t\tI was a member of the <a href=\"http://www.lti.cs.cmu.edu/\">Language Technologies Institute</a>.
