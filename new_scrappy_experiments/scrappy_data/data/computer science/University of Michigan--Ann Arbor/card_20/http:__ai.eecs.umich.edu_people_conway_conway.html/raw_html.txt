face=\"Times New Roman\">Introduction:</font></p>\n\t<p align=\"left\" style=\"margin-top: 8px; margin-bottom: 0\">\n\t<font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"2\">During the early 2010's, I began sketching \n\treflections on my experiences in engineering. I'd learned many lessons \n\tduring my work at \n\t<a href=\"http://ai.eecs.umich.edu/people/conway/Memoirs/ACS/Lynn_Conway_ACS_Reminiscences.pdf\" class=\"auto-style40\">IBM-ACS in the 1960's</a> \n\tand the\n\t<a href=\"http://ai.eecs.umich.edu/people/conway/Memoirs/VLSI/Lynn_Conway_VLSI_Reminiscences.pdf\" class=\"auto-style40\">'Mead-Conway' VLSI \n\trevolution in the 1970's</a>.&nbsp; I hoped to illuminate those experiences \n\tin memoirs before \n\ttime ran out, believing lessons-learned back then might prove useful to young engineers \n\tin the future.&nbsp;</font></p>\n\t<p align=\"left\" style=\"margin-top: 8px; margin-bottom: 0\">\n\t<font face=\"Times New Roman\" size=\"2\">Times had also changed enough to \n\tseriously begin this work. The widespread internet-based trans-advocacy of the 00's \n\thad had great impact: The\n\t<a href=\"http://ai.eecs.umich.edu/people/conway/TS/Bailey/McHugh/McHugh%20on%20Transsexualism.htm\" class=\"auto-style40\">\n\tpronouncements of leading American psychiatrists</a> on gender variance\n\t<a href=\"http://gendermadness.com/index.html\" class=\"auto-style40\">were exposed</a> as \n\t'unsound'.&nbsp;\n\t<a href=\"http://transgenderlawcenter.org/archives/2773\" class=\"auto-style40\">Laws changed</a>,\n\t<a href=\"http://www.hrc.org/corporate-equality-index\" class=\"auto-style40\">employment \n\topportunities opened up</a>, and the political landscape brightened. Transpeople \n\temerged from the shadows, taking their rightful places in society. My \n\tlong-ago transition was no longer the 'elephant in the room', blocking people \n\tfrom seeing the career-story looming beyond. It was time to return to my intellectual roots in \n\tscience, mathematics and engineering, time to share what I can about such \n\tthings.</font></p>\n\t<p align=\"left\" class=\"auto-style132\">\n\t<a name=\"ACS\">&nbsp; <font size=\"2\">&nbsp;</font></a></p>\n\t<p class=\"auto-style133\">\n\t<font face=\"Times New Roman\">Lynn's </font>IBM-ACS Archive and Reminiscences:</p>\n\t<p class=\"auto-style26\" style=\"margin-top: 8px; margin-bottom: 0\">\n\t<font size=\"2\">I was hired by \n\t<a href=\"http://www.research.ibm.com/labs/watson/index.shtml\" class=\"auto-style40\">IBM Research</a> right out of graduate school and \n\tsoon joined what would become the \n\t<a href=\"http://www.computerhistory.org/collections/accession/102702369\" class=\"auto-style40\">IBM Advanced Computing Systems</a> \n\t(a pioneering\n\t<a href=\"http://www.computer.org/portal/web/pressroom/2010/conway\" class=\"auto-style40\">\n\tsupercomputer project</a>),&nbsp; \n\tjust as it was forming in 1965. It was a golden era in computer research, a \n\ttime when fundamental breakthroughs were being made across a wide front.\n\t</font></p>\n\t<p style=\"margin-top: 8px; margin-bottom: 0\" class=\"auto-style26\">\n\t<font size=\"2\">The well-distilled and highly codified results of that and \n\tsubsequent work, as contained in today\u2019s modern textbooks, give no clue as \n\tto how those breakthroughs \u2018came to be\u2019. Lost in those texts is all the excitement, the \n\tchallenge, the confusion, the camaraderie, the chaos and the fun \u2013 the \n\tfeeling of what it was really like to be there, at the frontier, at that \n\ttime. In this, my first foray into memoir writing, I hoped to bring \n\tsome of that thrill back to life. This effort was also essential preparation for \n\twriting the follow-on VLSI memoir. The reason was that I'd gained many valuable scientific and engineering insights while at ACS, and \n\thad drawn heavily upon those insights during my later VLSI research.\n\t</font></p>\n\t<p class=\"auto-style26\" style=\"margin-top: 8px; margin-bottom: 0\">\n\t<font size=\"2\">Many questions also lingered about what had happened \n\tat ACS.&nbsp; I wondered what \u2018inside-explanation\u2019 had been used within IBM to \n\trationalize the project's \n\tcancellation in 1968. Did the managers who killed the project not realize that my \n\t\"<a class=\"auto-style40\" href=\"http://ai.eecs.umich.edu/people/conway/ACS/DIS/DynInstSched-paper.html\">dynamic \n\tinstruction scheduling</a>\" (DIS) invention \n\thad been included in, and had greatly empowered, the machine design? \n\tThen too, why did IBM fail to patent DIS and fail to exploit it in their later computers? \n\tWas it possible that several rather influential ACS researchers \n\tdid not understand what DIS was? Much less how it worked, or who had \n\tinvented it? If so, how could \n\tthat be? And why was I fired so suddenly, once IBM\u2019s Corporate \n\tExecutive Committee (i.e.,\n\tIBM's President and \n\tCEO \n\t<a class=\"auto-style40\" href=\"http://www-03.ibm.com/ibm/history/exhibits/watsonjr/watsonjr_intro.html\">T. J. Watson, Jr.</a>) learned about my upcoming gender transition? That \n\tfiring seemed impulsively executed, as if in hot-anger. What on earth was that all about?\n\t</font></p>\n\t<p class=\"auto-style26\" style=\"margin-top: 8px; margin-bottom: 0\">\n\t<font size=\"2\">I launched an investigation in early 2011 to finally begin answering \n\tthose questions and more. I was fortunate to have access to extensive \n\t<a href=\"http://people.cs.clemson.edu/~mark/acs.html\" class=\"auto-style40\">ACS \n\thistorical files</a> compiled by \n\t<a href=\"http://people.cs.clemson.edu/~mark/\" class=\"auto-style40\">Mark Smotherman</a> of Clemson \n\tUniversity, along with\n\t<a href=\"http://ai.eecs.umich.edu/people/conway/ACS/Archive/ACSarchive.html\" class=\"auto-style40\">\n\tmy own archive of original ACS documents</a>, plus valuable evidence that \n\thad emerged onto the internet over the years. \n\tMy ACS colleague\n\t<a href=\"http://www.ncl.ac.uk/computing/people/profile/brian.randell\" class=\"auto-style40\">Brian Randell</a> also had many questions about the project, \n\twhich had been cancelled \n\t<a href=\"http://citeseerx.ist.psu.edu/viewdoc/download?doi=10.1.1.62.8690&amp;rep=rep1&amp;type=pdf\" class=\"auto-style40\">just as he had predicted \n\tearly-on in the project</a>. Collaborating closely via Skype, we happened \n\tupon and shared lots of \n\tadditional evidence, and began making sense out of it all. The findings so \n\tfar are fascinating indeed, as you\u2019ll discover in the resulting memoir. \n\tAmong other things,\n\t<a class=\"auto-style40\" href=\"http://ai.eecs.umich.edu/people/conway/Memoirs/ACS/Lynn_Conway_ACS_Reminiscences.pdf\">\n\twe uncovered</a> that\n\t<a class=\"auto-style40\" href=\"http://ai.eecs.umich.edu/people/conway/Memoirs/Talks/RSE/Life_In_Stealth_of_Microchip_Genius_The_Times.pdf\">\n\tT. J. Watson, Jr. was a rabid homophobe</a>.</font></p>\n\t<p class=\"auto-style26\" style=\"margin-top: 8px; margin-bottom: 0\">\n\t<font size=\"2\">While that work was underway, I was invited by John Lloyd to \n\tcontribute a chapter to a Festschrift honoring Brian on his 75th birthday. \n\tIt was published in November 2011.
