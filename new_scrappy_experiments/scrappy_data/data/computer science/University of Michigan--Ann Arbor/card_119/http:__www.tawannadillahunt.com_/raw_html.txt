class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t\t<p>I am an Assistant Professor at the University of Michigan\u2019s <a href=\"https://www.si.umich.edu/\">School of Information</a> (UMSI) and hold a courtesy appointment with the&nbsp;<a href=\"https://www.eecs.umich.edu/\">Electrical Engineering and Computer Science</a> (EECS) department. Before starting as an Assistant Professor,&nbsp;I was a Presidential Postdoctoral Fellow in UMSI from January 2013 \u2013 July 2014. I also lead the <a href=\"http://socialinnovations.us\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener noreferrer\">Social Innovations Group</a>&nbsp;at UMSI and I am a member of the&nbsp;<a href=\"https://michiganictd.org/\">Michigan ICTD</a>&nbsp;and <a href=\"http://misc.si.umich.edu/\">Michigan Interactive and Social Computing (MISC)</a> groups. My research spans Human-Computer Interaction and Ubiquitous Computing fields. I am primarily interested in identifying needs and opportunities to further explore how theories from the social sciences can be used to design technologies that have a positive impact on group and individual behavior.&nbsp;With the narrowing of the digital divide, the ubiquity of smart devices and mobile hotspots in common places in the U.S. (e.g., libraries, community centers, and even McDonald\u2019s) I see an urgent need to explore the use of these technologies for those that stand the most to gain from these resources. Therefore,&nbsp;I design, build, enhance and deploy innovative technologies that solve real-world problems, particularly in underserved communities.</p>\n<p>I hold a M.S. and Ph.D. in Human-Computer Interaction from Carnegie Mellon University,&nbsp;a M.S. in Computer Science from the Oregon Graduate Institute School of Science and Engineering (now a part of the Oregon Health and Science University in Portland, OR), and a&nbsp;B.S. in Computer Engineering from North Carolina State University.
