Selected as an ACM Distinguished Educator in 2012 and ACM\nDistinguished Speaker in 2019, he has won all four of the department's\ncomputer science teaching awards, and holds the record for the highest\nteaching effectiveness ratings in the history of several of the\ndepartment's courses.<p>He is a national leader in the \"CSforALL\" movement, bringing engaging\ncomputer science to students normally underrepresented in the\nfield. Locally, he serves as the CSforCA higher education\nco-chair. Thanks to four National Science Foundation grants, the\n\"Beauty and Joy of Computing (BJC)\" non-majors course he co-developed\nhas been shared with over 500 high school teachers. He is delighted to\nregularly have more than 50% female enrollment in BJC, with a high\nmark of 65% in the Spring of 2018, shattering the record at UC\nBerkeley for an intro computing course, and is among the highest in\nthe nation! He is humbled by the national exposure he and the course\nhave received in the New York Times, PBS NewsHour, NPR's All Things\nConsidered, USA Today, and the front pages of the San Jose Mercury\nNews and San Francisco Chronicle.</p><p>He has won the NCWIT Undergraduate Research Mentoring award, the UC\nBerkeley Unsung Hero award, the LPFI Lux award, the SAP Visionary\nMember award, and was chosen as a Google CS4HS Ambassador for his work\nto support teachers and diversify computing. He has served on the ACM\nEducation Board, the College Board Computer Science Principles\nDevelopment Committee, was the ACM SIGCSE Program co-chair in 2017,\nand the ACM SIGCSE Symposium co-chair in 2018. He was recently elected\nACM SIGCSE Vice-Chair for the 2019-2022 term. In 2019 it was announced\nhe was the most frequent SIGCSE author in their 50-year history, with\n*61* submissions of all kinds: papers, panels, workshops, posters,\netc.; second place had 42.\n                    </p></div>\n                            </section>\n\n            <section class=\"section publications\">\n                                                    <h2>Education</h2>\n                    <ul>\n                                                    <li>\n                                2000, Ph.D., Computer Science, UC Berkeley\n                            </li>\n                                                    <li>\n                                1995, M.S., Computer Science, UC Berkeley\n                            </li>\n                                                    <li>\n                                1990, B.S., Computer Science, MIT\n                            </li>\n                                                    <li>\n                                1990, B.S., Electrical Engineering, MIT\n                            </li>\n                                            </ul>\n                            </section>\n\n            <section class=\"section publications\">\n                                    <h2>Selected Publications</h2>\n                    <ul>\n                                                    <li>\n                                D.
