     <section class=\"section bio\">\n                                    <h2>Biography</h2>\n                    <div>\n                        <p>Michael I. Jordan is the Pehong Chen Distinguished Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science and the Department of Statistics at the University of California, Berkeley.</p> <p></p><p>His research interests bridge the computational, statistical, cognitive\nand biological sciences, and have focused in recent years on Bayesian\nnonparametric analysis, probabilistic graphical models, spectral\nmethods, kernel machines and applications to problems in distributed computing\nsystems, natural language processing, signal processing and statistical\ngenetics.  Prof. Jordan is a member of the National Academy\nof Sciences, a member of the National Academy of Engineering and a\nmember of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.  He is a\nFellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science.\nHe has been named a Neyman Lecturer and a Medallion Lecturer by the\nInstitute of Mathematical Statistics.  He received the IJCAI Research\nExcellence Award in 2016, the David E. Rumelhart Prize in 2015 and \nthe ACM/AAAI Allen Newell Award in 2009.  He is a Fellow of the AAAI, \nACM, ASA, CSS, IEEE, IMS, ISBA and SIAM.</p>\n                    </div>\n                            </section>\n\n            <section class=\"section publications\">\n                                                    <h2>Education</h2>\n                    <ul>\n                                                    <li>\n                                1985, Ph.D., Cognitive Science, UC San Diego\n                            </li>\n                                                    <li>\n                                1980, M.S., Mathematics, Arizona State University\n                            </li>\n                                                    <li>\n                                1978, B.S., Psychology, Louisiana State University\n                            </li>\n                                            </ul>\n                            </section>\n\n            <section class=\"section publications\">\n                                    <h2>Selected Publications</h2>\n                    <ul>\n                                                    <li>\n                                J.
