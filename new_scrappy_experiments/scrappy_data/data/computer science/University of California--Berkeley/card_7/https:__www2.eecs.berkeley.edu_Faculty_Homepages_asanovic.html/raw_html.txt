He received a PhD in Computer Science from UC\nBerkeley in 1998 then joined the faculty at MIT, receiving tenure in\n2005.  He returned to join the faculty at Berkeley in 2007.\nHis main research areas are computer architecture, VLSI design,\nparallel programming and operating system design. He is currently\nCo-Director of the Berkeley ADEPT lab tackling the challenge of\ndeploying custom silicon to meet new application demands.\nHe leads the free RISC-V ISA project, is Chairman of the\nRISC-V Foundation, and co-founded SiFive Inc. to support\ncommercial use of RISC-V processors.  He is also an Associate Director\nat the Berkeley Wireless Research Center. He\nreceived the NSF CAREER award, and is an ACM Fellow and an IEEE Fellow.\n                    </div>\n                            </section>\n\n            <section class=\"section publications\">\n                                                    <h2>Education</h2>\n                    <ul>\n                                                    <li>\n                                1998, PhD, Computer Science, UC Berkeley\n                            </li>\n                                                    <li>\n                                1987, BA, Electrical and Information Sciences, University of Cambridge, UK\n                            </li>\n                                            </ul>\n                            </section>\n\n            <section class=\"section publications\">\n                                    <h2>Selected Publications</h2>\n                    <ul>\n                                                    <li>\n                                M.
