Ken holds dual degrees in Electrical Engineering and Economics from the University of Pennsylvania (1984) and a PhD from Carnegie Mellon University (1990). He joined the UC Berkeley faculty in 1995 and is Professor of Industrial Engineering and Operations Research, with secondary appointments in EECS, the School of Information, Art Practice, and the UCSF Dept of Radiation Oncology. Ken and his co-authors have published over 200 peer-reviewed technical papers on algorithms for robotics, automation, and social information filtering, and he holds eight U.S. patents. He is Editor-in-Chief of the <em>IEEE Transactions on Automation Science and Engineering</em> (T-ASE), Co-Founder of the Berkeley Center for New Media, Co-Founder and CTO of Hybrid Wisdom Labs, Co-Founder of the Moxie Institute, and Founding Director of UC Berkeley's Art, Technology, and Culture Lecture Series.<p>Ken's art installations, based on his research, have been exhibited internationally at venues such as the Whitney Biennial, the Berkeley Art Museum, the SF Contemporary Jewish Museum, the Pompidou Center, the Buenos Aires Biennial, and the ICC in Tokyo. Ken has co-written three award-winning Sundance documentary films, \"The Tribe\", \"Yelp\", and \"Connected: An Autoblogography of Love, Death, and Technology.\" He is represented by the Catharine Clark Gallery in San Francisco.</p><p>Ken was awarded the Presidential Faculty Fellowship by President Clinton in 1995, the National Science Foundation Faculty Fellowship in 1994, the Joseph Engelberger Robotics Award in 2000, and was elected IEEE Fellow in 2005.\n                    </p></div>\n                            </section>\n\n            <section class=\"section publications\">\n                                                    <h2>Education</h2>\n                    <ul>\n                                                    <li>\n                                1990, PhD, Computer Science, Carnegie Mellon University\n                            </li>\n                                                    <li>\n                                1984, BSEE, Electrical Engineering, University of Pennsylvania\n                            </li>\n                                                    <li>\n                                1984, BSE, Economics, UPenn - Wharton\n                            </li>\n                                            </ul>\n                            </section>\n\n            <section class=\"section publications\">\n                                    <h2>Selected Publications</h2>\n                    <ul>\n                                                    <li>\n                                B.
