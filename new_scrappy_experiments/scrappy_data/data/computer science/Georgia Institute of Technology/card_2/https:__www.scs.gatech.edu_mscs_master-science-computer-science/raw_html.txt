     \n            \n            \n            <div class=\"no-sidebars\" id=\"content\">\n\n                \n                                                  <div class=\"region region-content block-count1 clearfix\">\n    <div id=\"block-system-main\" class=\"block block-system   block-region-weight-1 odd\">\n\n      \n  <div class=\"content block-body clearfix\">\n    <div id=\"node-11250\" class=\"node node-multipurpose-page node-full clearfix\" about=\"/future/masters/mscs\" typeof=\"sioc:Item foaf:Document\">\n\n  \n      <span property=\"dc:title\" content=\"Master of Science in Computer Science\" class=\"rdf-meta element-hidden\"></span>\n  \n  <div class=\"content\">\n    \n          <div class=\"content-row row-top clearfix\">\n                  <div class=\"body\">\n              <p><img alt=\"Woman working on her Master of Science in Computer Science\" src=\"/sites/default/files/images/computer-science_1.jpg\" style=\"height:714px; width:1000px\"></p>\n<p>The Master of Science in Computer Science (M.S. CS) program is a terminal degree program designed to prepare students for more highly productive careers in industry.</p>\n<p>Graduates receive the M.S. CS for completing one of three options in the program as described in the program of study. The program is designed for students who possess a bachelor's degree in computer science from an accredited institution. Students with a bachelor's degree in other than computer science are encouraged to apply as well, with the understanding that they may be required to complete remedial course work in addition to the requirements of the M.S. CS degree.</p>\n<h4>Admissions</h4>\n<p>Admission to the program is highly selective; there are many more qualified applicants than there are places in the program. The challenge for the College every year is to select a class from a highly qualified pool. The College looks for compelling reasons to admit candidates, and the statement of purpose, letters of recommendation, test scores and GPA are all reviewed carefully. Having a strong undergraduate background in computer science, including C programming, is highly recommended for applicants. The Admissions Committee welcomes additional pertinent information that will aid in making objective and informed decisions.</p>\n<p>Students are admitted to the master's program only once a year. The application deadline is Feb. 1 for admission to the following fall semester.</p>\n<p>Learn important information about our admissions process on our Admissions Requirements page.</p>\n<p class=\"highlight-link-yellow\"><a href=\"/academics/degree-programs/masters/computer-science/admissionreqs\">Admissions Requirements</a></p>\n<h4>Program rules</h4>\n<ul>\n<li>GPA \u2013 Students must achieve a cumulative grade point average of at least 3.0 to graduate, and no course grades below C will be allowed to count toward graduation. All grades labeled below as core and elective courses within your specialization must be B or better.</li>\n<li>No course may be used to satisfy the requirements of two degrees. In addition, no graduate credit will be given for CS courses with a number lower than 4140.</li>\n<li>A maximum of 6 hours may be taken at the 4000-level and/or with a subject code other than CS or CSE. See exceptions under the Project and Thesis options below.</li>\n<li>Pass-Fail and Audit \u2013 All courses applied to the M.S. CS degree must be taken for letter grade, other than thesis hours.</li>\n<li>The maximum total credit hours of Special Problems (CS or CSE 89xx) that may be applied toward the M.S. CS degree is 3. These courses must be within the CoC.</li>\n<li>There is no maximum number of Special Topics (CS or CSE 88xx) courses that may be used towards the degree.</li>\n<li>Each student must complete the requirements for one specialization.</li>\n</ul>\n<p class=\"jump-link\"><a href=\"https://www.cc.gatech.edu/equivalent-courses\">Equivalent Courses</a></p>\n<h4>Program Options</h4>\n<p>Students may choose from one of the following three options in pursuing the M.S. CS.</p>\n<h6>Course Option</h6>\n<ul>\n<li>30&nbsp;hours of course work (no M.S. project or thesis hours)</li>\n<li>Total course credit hours required: 30</li>\n<li>Minimum CS/CSE course credit hours: 24</li>\n<li>Minimum CS/CSE course credit hours at the graduate (6000-8000) level: 24</li>\n<li>Minimum total credit hours at the 6000-8000 level: 24</li>\n</ul>\n<h6>Project Option</h6>\n<ul>\n<li>21&nbsp;hours of course work and a 9-hour project. The student must obtain advance approval of the project proposal by the faculty advisor and&nbsp;M.S. CS&nbsp;coordinator. See your academic advisor for more information.</li>\n<li>Total credit hours required: 30</li>\n<li>M.S. CS&nbsp;project hours (CS 6999): 9</li>\n<li>Total course credit hours: 21</li>\n<li>Minimum CS/CSE course hours required: 15*</li>\n<li>Minimum CS/CSE course credit hours at the graduate (6000-8000) level: 15*</li>\n</ul>\n<h6>Thesis Option</h6>\n<ul>\n<li>18 hours of course work and a 12-hour thesis. The student must obtain advance approval of the thesis proposal by the faculty advisor and MSCS coordinator. See your academic advisor for more information about the thesis process.</li>\n<li>Total credit hours required: 30</li>\n<li>M.S. CS thesis hours (CS 7000): 12</li>\n<li>Minimum CS/CSE course hours required: 15*</li>\n<li>Minimum CS/CSE course credit hours at the graduate (6000-8000) level: 15*</li>\n</ul>\n<p><em>*May not include MS project or thesis hours.</em></p>\n<p><a href=\"/three-year-course-outline\" target=\"_blank\"><strong>Click here to view the Graduate Course Offering Plan for Spring 2019 to Fall 2021.</strong></a></p>\n<p><a href=\"/academics/college-advising/regdates\" target=\"_blank\"><strong>Click here to view Student Registration Dates for Spring 2020.</strong></a></p>\n<h5><strong>Specializations</strong></h5>\n<p>Students in the M.S. CS program choose from 11&nbsp;areas of specialization. This allows students to better customize their degree program. To find out which courses are required for each specialization, <a href=\"/ms-cs-specializations\">visit our M.S. CS Specializations page to explore the possibilities.</a>&nbsp;</p>\n<h5>Old Curriculum</h5>\n<p><a href=\"/academics/degree-programs/masters/computer-science/old-curriculum\">Click here to view the previous M.S. CS course curriculum.</a></p>\n          </div>\n                          \n      </div>\n        \n        \n        \n  </div>\n\n  \n  \n</div>  </div>\n  \n</div>\n  </div>\n                                            </div><!-- /#content -->\n\n            \n            \n            \n        </div>\n    </section><!-- /#main -->\n\n    <div id=\"contentinfo\" aria-label=\"Footers\">\n        <!-- #superfooter/ -->\n        <div class=\"superfooter-trigger-wrapper clearfix\"><a class=\"js__superfooter-trigger collapsed\" href=\"#superfooter\" id=\"superfooter-trigger\">Resources</a></div><section id=\"superfooter\" class=\"superfooter-configurable collapsible\"><div class=\"row clearfix\"><div class=\"superfooter-resource-links\" id=\"gt-default-resource-links\" role=\"navigation\" aria-labelledby=\"gt-default-resource-links-title\">\n    <h4 class=\"title\" id=\"gt-default-resource-links-title\">Georgia Tech Resources</h4>\n    <ul class=\"menu\" id=\"gt-default-resources\">\n      <li><a href=\"http://www.gatech.edu/offices-and-departments\">Offices &amp;
