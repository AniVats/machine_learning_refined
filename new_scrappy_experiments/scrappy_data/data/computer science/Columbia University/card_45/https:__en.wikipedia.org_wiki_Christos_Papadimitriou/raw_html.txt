class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a href=\"/w/index.php?title=Christos_Papadimitriou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1\" title=\"Edit section: Education\">edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Papadimitriou studied at the <a href=\"/wiki/National_Technical_University_of_Athens\" title=\"National Technical University of Athens\">National Technical University of Athens</a>, where in 1972 he received his <a href=\"/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts\" title=\"Bachelor of Arts\">Bachelor of Arts</a> degree in <a href=\"/wiki/Electrical_Engineering\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Electrical Engineering\">Electrical Engineering</a>. He continued to study at <a href=\"/wiki/Princeton_University\" title=\"Princeton University\">Princeton University</a>, where he received his <a href=\"/wiki/Master_of_Science\" title=\"Master of Science\">MS</a> in Electrical Engineering in 1974 and his <a href=\"/wiki/PhD\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"PhD\">PhD</a> in Electrical Engineering and <a href=\"/wiki/Computer_Science\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Computer Science\">Computer Science</a> in 1976.\n</p>\n<h2><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Career\">Career</span><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a href=\"/w/index.php?title=Christos_Papadimitriou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2\" title=\"Edit section: Career\">edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Papadimitriou has taught at <a href=\"/wiki/Harvard\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Harvard\">Harvard</a>, <a href=\"/wiki/MIT\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"MIT\">MIT</a>, the <a href=\"/wiki/National_Technical_University_of_Athens\" title=\"National Technical University of Athens\">National Technical University of Athens</a>, <a href=\"/wiki/Stanford\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Stanford\">Stanford</a>, <a href=\"/wiki/UCSD\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"UCSD\">UCSD</a>, <a href=\"/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley\" title=\"University of California, Berkeley\">University of California, Berkeley</a> and is currently the Donovan Family Professor of Computer Science at Columbia University.\n</p><p>Papadimitriou co-authored a paper on <a href=\"/wiki/Pancake_sorting\" title=\"Pancake sorting\">pancake sorting</a> with <a href=\"/wiki/Bill_Gates\" title=\"Bill Gates\">Bill Gates</a>, then a Harvard undergraduate.  Papadimitriou recalled \"Two years later, I called to tell him our paper had been accepted to a fine math journal. He sounded eminently disinterested. He had moved to Albuquerque, New Mexico to run a small company writing code for microprocessors, of all things. I remember thinking: 'Such a brilliant kid. What a waste.'\" The company was <a href=\"/wiki/Microsoft\" title=\"Microsoft\">Microsoft</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-8\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-8\">[8]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Papadimitriou co-authored \"The Complexity of Computing a Nash Equilibrium\" with his students <a href=\"/wiki/Constantinos_Daskalakis\" title=\"Constantinos Daskalakis\">Constantinos Daskalakis</a> and Paul W. Goldberg, for which they received the 2008 <a href=\"/wiki/Kalai_Prize\" title=\"Kalai Prize\">Kalai Game Theory and Computer Science Prize</a> from the <a href=\"/wiki/Game_Theory_Society\" title=\"Game Theory Society\">Game Theory Society</a> for \"the best paper at the interface of game theory and computer science\",<sup id=\"cite_ref-9\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-9\">[9]</a></sup> in particular \"for its key conceptual and technical contributions\";<sup id=\"cite_ref-10\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-10\">[10]</a></sup> and the Outstanding Paper Prize from the <a href=\"/wiki/Society_for_Industrial_and_Applied_Mathematics\" title=\"Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics\">Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics</a>.\n</p><p>In 2001, Papadimitriou was inducted as a <a href=\"/wiki/Fellow\" title=\"Fellow\">Fellow</a> of the <a href=\"/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery\" title=\"Association for Computing Machinery\">Association for Computing Machinery</a> and in 2002 he was awarded the <a href=\"/wiki/Knuth_Prize\" title=\"Knuth Prize\">Knuth Prize</a>. He became fellow of the U.S. <a href=\"/wiki/National_Academy_of_Engineering\" title=\"National Academy of Engineering\">National Academy of Engineering</a> for contributions to complexity theory, database theory, and <a href=\"/wiki/Combinatorial_optimization\" title=\"Combinatorial optimization\">combinatorial optimization</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-11\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-11\">[11]</a></sup>  In 2009 he was elected to the US <a href=\"/wiki/United_States_National_Academy_of_Sciences\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"United States National Academy of Sciences\">National Academy of Sciences</a>. During the 36th <a href=\"/wiki/International_Colloquium_on_Automata,_Languages_and_Programming\" title=\"International Colloquium on Automata, Languages and Programming\">International Colloquium on Automata, Languages and Programming</a> (ICALP 2009), there was a special event honoring Papadimitriou's contributions to computer science.<sup id=\"cite_ref-12\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-12\">[12]</a></sup> In 2012, he, along with Elias Koutsoupias, was awarded the <a href=\"/wiki/G%C3%B6del_Prize\" title=\"G\u00f6del Prize\">G\u00f6del Prize</a> for their joint work on the concept of the <a href=\"/wiki/Price_of_anarchy\" title=\"Price of anarchy\">price of anarchy</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-SIGACT-Godel_13-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-SIGACT-Godel-13\">[13]</a></sup>\n</p><p>Papadimitriou is the author of the textbook <i>Computational Complexity</i>, one of the most widely used textbooks in the field of <a href=\"/wiki/Computational_complexity_theory\" title=\"Computational complexity theory\">computational complexity theory</a>. He has also co-authored the textbook <i>Algorithms</i> (2008) with Sanjoy Dasgupta and <a href=\"/wiki/Umesh_Vazirani\" title=\"Umesh Vazirani\">Umesh Vazirani</a>, and the graphic novel <i><a href=\"/wiki/Logicomix\" title=\"Logicomix\">Logicomix</a></i> (2009)<sup id=\"cite_ref-14\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-14\">[14]</a></sup> with <a href=\"/wiki/Apostolos_Doxiadis\" title=\"Apostolos Doxiadis\">Apostolos Doxiadis</a>.\n</p><p>His name was listed in the 19th position on the <a href=\"/wiki/CiteSeer\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"CiteSeer\">CiteSeer</a> search engine academic database and digital library<sup class=\"noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact\" style=\"white-space:nowrap;\">[<i><a href=\"/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed\" title=\"Wikipedia:Citation needed\"><span title=\"This claim needs a source (January 2017)\">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Honors_and_awards\">Honors and awards</span><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a href=\"/w/index.php?title=Christos_Papadimitriou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3\" title=\"Edit section: Honors and awards\">edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1997, Papadimitriou received a <a href=\"/wiki/Doctorate_honoris_causa\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Doctorate honoris causa\">doctorate <i>honoris causa</i></a> from the <a href=\"/wiki/ETH_Zurich\" title=\"ETH Zurich\">ETH Zurich</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-15\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-15\">[15]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2011, Papadimitriou received a <a href=\"/wiki/Doctorate_honoris_causa\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Doctorate honoris causa\">doctorate <i>honoris causa</i></a> from the <a href=\"/wiki/National_Technical_University_of_Athens\" title=\"National Technical University of Athens\">National Technical University of Athens</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-16\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-16\">[16]</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2013, Papadimitriou received a <a href=\"/wiki/Doctorate_honoris_causa\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Doctorate honoris causa\">doctorate <i>honoris causa</i></a> from the <a
