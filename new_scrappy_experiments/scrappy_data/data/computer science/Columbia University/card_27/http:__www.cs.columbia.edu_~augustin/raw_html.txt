of computing and how we deal with information, economic and environmental issues. In my research I model mobile and social dynamics to develop new algorithms that improve computing applications, eventually building information networks that make our decisions easier and better.  -->\nI design algorithms and mathematical analysis of networks. The goal of my research is to reconcile the benefits of leveraging personal data and social networks with a commitment to fairness and privacy.\n\nLatest results of my research address transparency in personalization, fairness in personal data markets, efficiency of crowdsourced content curation, and privacy of users across several domains.\n</p>\n\n<!-- <h2>How do you do that?</h2> -->\n\n<p align=\"justify\" class=\"firstpara\">\n<!-- My technical background is in probabilistic modeling, distributed algorithms and network measurement. Work in my research area typically involves (1) collecting and analyzing traces to understand users' behavior, in particular when it comes to their mobility or communication exchange; (2) seeing how it impacts the performance of simple algorithms that use only a small amount of information about the users and the network state; (3) exploring the ramifications, in particular when an application deals with competing interests, and the conditions under which new algorithmic design can be deployed. -->\n</p> \n\n<h2>Some Recent Papers (<a href=\"https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&amp;user=Hb6J54cAAAAJ&amp;view_op=list_works&amp;sortby=pubdate\">complete chronological list</a>)</h2>\n\n<p class=\"firstpara\">\n</p><ul>\n\t<li> \n\tM.
