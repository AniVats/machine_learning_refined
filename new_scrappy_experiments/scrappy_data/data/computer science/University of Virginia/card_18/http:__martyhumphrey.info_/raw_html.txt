   <a href=\"//www.martyhumphrey.info/publication/2019ParBio/index.html\" itemprop=\"url\">Leveraging the Cloud for Intelligent Clinical Data Registries (ACM-BCB'19)</a>\n            </h3>\n\n            <div class=\"pub-abstract\" itemprop=\"text\">\n                \n                    Public cloud platforms provide an amazing set of capabilities, but it can be an overwhelming challenge to create a design, implementation, and deployment that properly leverages today\u2019s existing public cloud capabilities while not precluding the use of near-future new services and infrastructure. We tackle this challenge in the context of clinical data registries, and create Cloud-based Patient Outcomes Platform (CPOP), our scalable public cloud application for clinical patient data. Doctors are able to visualize collected medical data in different chart formats and patients are able to check their data and submit medical survey forms. The specific domain of interest in this paper is Chronic Rhinosinusitis (CRS), a largely under-recognized chronic disease in our society. \n                \n            </div>\n\n            <div itemprop=\"url\">CloudInsight: Utilizing a Council of Experts to Predict Future Cloud Application Workloads (IEEE Cloud 2018)</a>\n            </h3>\n\n            <div class=\"pub-abstract\" itemprop=\"text\">\n                \n                    Many predictive approaches have been proposed to overcome the limitations of reactive autoscaling on clouds. These approaches leverage workload predictors that are usually targeted for a particular workload pattern and can fail to handle real-world cloud workloads whose patterns may be unknown a priori, may dynamically change over time, or may be irregular. The result is that resources are frequently under- and overprovisioned. To address this problem, we create a novel cloud workload prediction framework called CloudInsight, leveraging the combined power of multiple workload predictors that collectively provide a 'council of experts'.\n                \n            </div>\n\n            <div class=\"pub-authors\" itemprop=\"author\">\n                \n                In Kee Kim, Wei Wang, Yanjun Qi, Marty Humphrey\n                \n            </div>\n\n            <div class=\"pub-publication\">\n                \n                    In <em>10th IEEE International Conference on Cloud Computing (Cloud 2018)</em>, July 2 - Orchestra is designed to address 'resource storms' caused by sudden executions of the background services on the cloud instances. \n                \n            </div>\n\n            <div class=\"pub-authors\" itemprop=\"author\">\n                \n                In Kee Kim, Jinho Hwang, Wei Wang, Marty Humphrey\n                \n            </div>\n\n            <div class=\"pub-publication\">\n                \n                    In <em>17th IEEE International Symposium on Parallel and Distributed Computing (IEEE ISPDC 2018)</em>, June 25 - 28, 2018, Geneva, Switzerland\n                \n            </div>\n\n            <div class=\"pub-links\">\n                \n\n\n\n<a class=\"btn btn-primary btn-outline btn-xs\" href=\"//www.martyhumphrey.info/publication/2018ISPDC/index.html\">Details</a>\n\n\n<a class=\"btn btn-primary btn-outline btn-xs\" href=\"//www.martyhumphrey.info/pdf/Orchestra_ISPDC2018.pdf\">PDF</a>\n\n\n\n\n<a class=\"btn btn-primary btn-outline btn-xs\" href=\"//www.martyhumphrey.info/project/cloud-autoscaling/\">Project</a>\n\n\n\n            </div>\n\n        </div>\n    </div>\n</div>\n\n                \n                <div class=\"pub-list-item\" itemscope=\"\" itemtype=\"http://schema.org/CreativeWork\">\n    <div class=\"row\">\n        \n        <div class=\"col-md-12\">\n        \n\n            <h3 class=\"article-title\" itemprop=\"name\">\n                <a href=\"//www.martyhumphrey.info/publication/2017eScience/index.html\" itemprop=\"url\">Hunting Data Rogues at Scale: Data Quality Control for Observational Data in Research Infrastructures (eScience 2017)</a>\n            </h3>\n\n            <div class=\"pub-abstract\" itemprop=\"text\">\n                \n                    Data quality control is one of the most time consuming activities within Research Infrastructures (RIs), especially when involving observational data and multiple data providers. Oct 24-27 2017\n                \n            </div>\n\n            <div class=\"pub-links\">\n                \n\n\n\n<a class=\"btn btn-primary btn-outline btn-xs\" href=\"//www.martyhumphrey.info/publication/2017eScience/index.html\">Details</a>\n\n\n<a class=\"btn btn-primary btn-outline btn-xs\" href=\"https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/8109169/\">PDF</a>\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n            </div>\n\n        </div>\n    </div>\n</div>\n\n                \n                <div class=\"pub-list-item\" itemscope=\"\" itemtype=\"http://schema.org/CreativeWork\">\n    <div class=\"row\">\n        \n        <div class=\"col-md-12\">\n        \n\n            <h3 class=\"article-title\" itemprop=\"name\">\n                <a href=\"//www.martyhumphrey.info/publication/2016UCC/index.html\" itemprop=\"url\">Experiences Creating a Framework for Smart Traffic Control using AWS IOT (UCC 2016)</a>\n            </h3>\n\n            <div class=\"pub-abstract\" itemprop=\"text\">\n                \n                    While early public cloud IoT success stories have focused on smaller-scale scenarios such as connected houses, it is unclear to what extent these new public cloud mechanisms and abstractions are suitable and effective for larger-scale and/or scientific scenarios, which often have a different set of constraints or requirements. This paper addresses the challenge of implementing a scalable IoT infrastructure testbed in the public cloud for scientific experimentation.  The system created is for dynamic vehicle traffic control based on vehicle volumes/patterns and weather conditions.  We find that while AWS IoT performance and performance scalability are likely to meet the requirements of many next-generation scientific IoT use-cases, manageability/modifications of a scientific IoT scenario can be challenging for moderate- to large-scale deployments.\n                \n            </div>\n\n            <div class=\"pub-authors\" itemprop=\"author\">\n                \n
