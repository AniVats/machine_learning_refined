First, the development of block chain based systems for distributed computation (e.g. Ethereum) will lead to an unprecedented ability to decentralize the allocation of resources. Second, the coming introduction of self-driving, connected vehicles will lead to a once in a generation opportunity to revamp traffic infrastructure. Third, the move to a \"smart\" grid for the distribution of electricity and intelligent appliances that can respond to pricing information will allow for a degree of sensitivity to incentives that, up to now, has been impossible in energy markets. I have been involved in research in each of these three areas. Please look at my <a href=\"research.html\">publications</a> for more information.</p>\n\n\t  <p>Longer term, I am very interested in machine learning questions in settings where the agents generating the data have incentives to <i>strategically manipulate the data</i>.
