Security 2020: Hybrid Batch Attacks</a></h2>\n<div class=\"post-metadata\">\n  <span class=\"post-date\">\n    <time datetime=\"2019-12-14 00:00:00 +0000 UTC\" itemprop=\"datePublished\">14 December href=\"//jeffersonswheel.org/tags/adversarial-machine-learning\">adversarial machine learning</a>,</span> \n    \n    <a class=\"post-tag\" href=\"//jeffersonswheel.org/tags/fnu-suya\">Fnu Suya</a>, \n    \n    <a class=\"post-tag\" href=\"//jeffersonswheel.org/tags/yuan-tian\">Yuan Tian</a>, \n    \n    <a class=\"post-tag\" href=\"//jeffersonswheel.org/tags/jianfeng-chi\">Jianfeng Chi</a>, \n   \n\n<h2 id=\"finding-black-box-adversarial-examples-with-limited-queries\">Finding Black-box Adversarial Examples with Limited Queries</h2>\n\n<p>Black-box attacks generate adversarial examples (AEs) against deep\nneural networks with only API access to the victim model.</p>\n\n<p>Existing black-box attacks can be grouped into two main categories:</p>\n\n<ul>\n<li><p><strong>Transfer Attacks</strong> use white-box attacks on local models to find\ncandidate adversarial examples that transfer to the target model.</p></li>\n\n<li><p><strong>Optimization Attacks</strong> use queries to the target model and apply\noptimization techniques to search for adversarial examples.</p></li>\n</ul>\n\n<h3 id=\"hybrid-attack\">Hybrid Attack</h3>\n\n<p>We propose a <em>hybrid attack</em> that combines transfer and optimization attacks:</p>\n\n<ol>\n<li><p>Transfer Attack \u2192 Optimization Attack \u2014 take candidate adversarial examples of the local models of transfer attacks as the starting points for optimization attacks.</p></li>\n\n<li><p>Optimization Attack \u2192 Transfer Attack \u2014 intermediate query results from the optimization attacks are used to fine-tune the local models of transfer attacks.</p></li>\n</ol>\n\n<p></p><center>\n<a href=\"/images/usenix2020/hybridattack.png\"><img src=\"/images/usenix2020/hybridattack.png\" width=\"60%\" align=\"center\"></a>\n</center><p></p>\n\n<p>We validate effectiveness of the hybrid attack over the baseline on three benchmark datasets: MNIST, CIFAR10, ImageNet. In this post, we only show the results of <a href=\"https://arxiv.org/abs/1805.11770\">AutoZOOM</a> as the selected optimization method. More results of other attacks can be found in the <a href=\"../docs/hybrid_attack.pdf\">paper</a>.</p>\n\n<h2 id=\"local-adversarial-examples-are-useful-transfer-rarr-optimization\">Local Adversarial Examples are Useful (Transfer \u2192 Optimization)</h2>\n\n<p>Below, we compare the performance of AutoZOOM attack when it starts\nfrom 1) the local adversarial examples, and 2) the original\npoints. Here, we report results for targeted attacks on normal (i.e.,\nnon-robust) models:</p>\n\n<p></p><center><a href=\"/images/usenix2020/local_candidate_results.png\"><img src=\"/images/usenix2020/local_candidate_results.png\" width=\"65%\" align=\"center\"></a></center><p></p>\n\n<p>Local AEs can substantially boost the performance of optimization\nattacks, but when the same attack is used against <a href=\"https://github.com/MadryLab/cifar10_challenge\">robust\nmodels</a>, the improvement is small:</p>\n\n<p></p><center><a href=\"<img src=\" images=\"\" usenix2020=\"\" normal_model_fails.png\"=\"\"><img src=\"/images/usenix2020/normal_model_fails.png\" width=\"65%\" align=\"center\"></a></center><p></p>\n\n<p>This ineffectiveness appears to stem from differences in the attack\nspace of normal and robust models. Therefore, to improve effectiveness\nagainst robust target model, we use robust local models to produce the\ntransfer candidates for starting the optimization attacks. The figure\nbelow compares impact of normal and robust local models when attacking\nthe robust target model:</p>\n\n<p></p><center><a href=\"/images/usenix2020/local_model_comparison.png\"><img src=\"/images/usenix2020/local_model_comparison.png\" width=\"60%\" align=\"center\"></a></center><p></p>\n\n<h2 id=\"tuning-with-byproduces-doesn-t-help-much-optimization-rarr-transfer\">Tuning with Byproduces Doesn\u2019t Help Much (Optimization \u2192 Transfer)</h2>\n\n<p>Below, we compare the performance of AutoZOOM attack on MNIST normal\nmodel when the local models are 1) fine-tuned during the attack\nprocess, and 2) kept static:</p>\n\n<p></p><center><a href=\"/images/usenix2020/fine_tune_results.png\"><img src=\"/images/usenix2020/fine_tune_results.png\" width=\"60%\" align=\"center\"></a></center><p></p>\n\n<p>Tuining local models using byproducts from the optimization attack\nimproves the query efficiency. However, for more complex datasets\n(e.g., CIFAR10), we observe degradation in the attack performance by\nfine-tuning (check Table 6 in the <a href=\"../docs/hybrid_attack.pdf\">paper</a>).</p>\n\n<h2 id=\"batch-attacks\">Batch Attacks</h2>\n\n<p>We consider a <strong>batch attack</strong> scenario: adversaries have limited\nnumber of queries and want to maximize the number of adversarial\nexamples found within the limit. This is a more realistic way to\nevaluate attacks for most adversarial purposes, then just looking at\nthe average cost to attack each seed in a large pool of seeds.</p>\n\n<p>The number of queries required for attacking a specific seed varies\ngreatly across seeds:</p>\n\n<p></p><center><a href=\"/images/usenix2020/query_variance.png\"><img src=\"../images/usenix2020/query_variance.png\" width=\"80%\" align=\"center\"></a></center><p></p>\n\n<p>Based on this observation, we propose <strong>two-phase strategy</strong> to prioritize easy seeds for the <strong>hybrid attack</strong>:</p>\n\n<ol>\n<li><p>In the first phase, the likely-to-transfer seeds are prioritized\nbased on their PGD-steps taken to attack the local models. The\ncandidate adversarial example for seed seed is attempted in order to\nfind all the direct transfers.</p></li>\n\n<li><p>In the second phase, the remaining seeds are prioritized based on\ntheir target loss value with respect to the target model.</p></li>\n</ol>\n\n<p>To validate effectievness of the two-phase strategy, we compare to two seed prioritization strategies:</p>\n\n<ul>\n<li><p><strong>Retroactive Optimal</strong>: a non-realizable attack that assumes adversaries already know the exact number of queries to attack each seed (before the attack starts) and can prioritize seeds by their actual query cost. This provides an lower bound on the query cost for an optimal strategy.</p></li>\n\n<li><p><strong>Random:</strong> this is a baseline strategy where seeds are prioritized in random order (this is the stragety assumed in most works where the adverage costs are reported).</p></li>\n</ul>\n\n<p>Results for the AutoZOOM attack on a normal ImageNet model are shown below:</p>\n\n<p></p><center><img src=\"../images/usenix2020/batch_attack_results.png\" width=\"60%\" align=\"center\"></center><p></p>\n\n<p>Our two-phase strategy performs closely to the retroactive optimal\nstrategy and outpeforms random baseline significantly: with same\nnumber of query limit, two-phase strategy finds significantly more\nadversarial examples comapred to the random baseline, and is closer to\nthe retroactive optimal case. (See the paper for more experimental\nresults and variations on the prioritization strategy.)</p>\n\n<h3 id=\"main-takeaways\">Main Takeaways</h3>\n\n<ul>\n<li><p><strong>Transfer \u2192 Optimization:</strong> local adversarial examples can generally be used to boost optimization attacks. One caveat is, against robust target model, hybrid attack is more effective with robust local models.</p></li>\n\n<li><p><strong>Transfer \u2192 Optimization:</strong> fine-tuning local models is only helpful for small scale dataset (e.g., MNIST) and fails to generalize to more complex datasets. It is an open question whether we can make the fine-tuning process work for complex datasets.</p></li>\n\n<li><p><strong>Prioritizing seeds</strong> based on two-phase strategy for the hybrid attack can significantly improve its query efficiency in batch attack scenario.</p></li>\n</ul>\n\n<p>Our results make the case that it is important to evaluate both\nattacks and defenses with a more realistic adversary model than just\nlooking at the average cost to attack a seed over a large pool of\nseeds. When an adversary only need to find a small number of\nadversarial examples, and has access to a large pool of potential\nseeds to attack (of equal value to the adversary), then the effective\ncosts of a successful attack can be orders of magnitude lower than\nwhat would be projected assuming an adversary who cannot prioritize\nseeds to attack.</p>\n\n<h2 id=\"paper\">Paper</h2>\n\n<p><a href=\"https://fsuya.org\">Fnu Suya</a>, <a href=\"https://www.linkedin.com/in/jianfeng-chi-001b25133/\">Jianfeng Chi</a>, <a href=\"https://www.cs.virginia.edu/~evans/\">David Evans</a> and <a href=\"https://www.ytian.info\">Yuan Tian</a>. <a href=\"https://arxiv.org/pdf/1908.07000.pdf\"><em>Hybrid Batch Attacks: Finding Black-box\nAdversarial Examples with Limited Queries</em></a>. [<a href=\"/docs/hybrid_attack.pdf\">PDF</a>]&nbsp;[<a href=\"https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.07000\">arXiv</a>]</p>\n\n<h2 id=\"code\">Code</h2>\n\n<p><a href=\"https://github.com/suyeecav/Hybrid-Attack\">https://github.com/suyeecav/Hybrid-Attack</a></p>\n\n<p>In this repository, we provide the source code to reproduce the results in the paper. In addition, we believe our hybrid attack framework can (potentially) help boost the performance of new optimization attacks. Therefore, in the repository, we also provide tutorials to incorporate new optimization attacks into the hybrid attack framework.</p>\n\n      </div>\n<hr class=\"post-separator\">\n\n    \n    <h2><a After a short break,\nKavitha Cardoza moderated a very interesting panel discussion. A\nrecording of the talk and rest of the event is supposed to be\navailable to Great Courses Plus subscribers. No one has a job as this kind of match-maker today \u2013 and lots of the jobs the other kind of match-maker have also been taken over by machines.\n  </p></div>\n</div><p></p>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:15px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-14 medium-7\">\nAnother example is automated teller machine. ATMs automated many of\nthe roles previously done by human bank tellers.\n<p></p><p>\nATM Machines didn\u2019t eliminate jobs for bank tellers \u2013 actually made\nmore, since they make banking cheaper and more accessible, and meant\nbanks could open more branches which still needed to hire tellers for\nthe more interesting and complicated banking activities.\n</p><p>\nWe\u2019ve seen many transformations like this, and many tasks that could\nonce only be done by humans are now routinely automated. Although past\ntechnological advances have caused massive disruption and pain for\nmany individuals, as a species on the whole, these advances have not\ndiminished overall human employment, and there shouldn\u2019t be any doubt\nthat on the whole, technological and scientific program have made all\nof our lives tremendously better.\n  </p></div>\n  <div class=\"column small-10 medium-5\">\n  <br><br>\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/bankautomat.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/bankautomat.png\"></a>\n  <div class=\"midcaption\">\n  Source: James Pethokoukis, <a href=\"https://www.aei.org/economics/what-atms-bank-tellers-rise-robots-and-jobs/\"><em>What the story of ATMs and bank tellers reveals about the \u2018rise of the robots\u2019 and jobs</em></a>, based on <a href=\"https://www.amazon.com/Learning-Doing-Connection-between-Innovation/dp/0300195664\">James Bessen's book</a>.\n</div>\n\n<p></p></div><p></p>\n\n<p></p></div><p></p>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <center>\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide05.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide05.png\" width=\"80%\"></a>\n  <div class=\"midcaption\">\nGraph based on data from <a href=\"https://ourworldindata.org/employment-in-agriculture\"><em>Our World in Data</em></a>.\n  </div>\n</center>\n<br>\n<p>\nOne way to measure that progress is what fraction of our workforce is needed to feed us. \n</p>\n<p>\nAs recently as 150 years ago, nearly everyone worked in agriculture, and we still couldn\u2019t produce enough food to feed everyone. Now, only about 1 out of 75 people work in farming, and we are so ridiculously productive in producing food with we can burn about 1/3 of it as fuel.\n</p>\n<p>\nSo, if we were driven as a species to avoid work once our subsistence needs are met, we shouldn\u2019t be working 8 hours or more a day.\n</p>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\">\n  <div class=\"column small-14 medium-7\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide06.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide06.png\"></a>\n  <div class=\"midcaption\">\n  <em>If the ordinary wage-earner worked four hours a day, there would be enough for everybody and no unemployment... This is what traditional\ncomputing has done. \n</p>\n<p> \nMore recently, Steve Jobs talked about computers as <a href=\"https://www.youtube.com/watch?time_continue=3&amp;v=Pm_WAWZNbdA&amp;feature=emb_logo\">bicycles for the mind</a>.\n  </p></div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\">\n  <div class=\"column small-10 medium-5\">\nWe have machines now that can do any computation we understand well enough to describe in simple steps quadrillions of times faster than humans can do it. We can also build computing systems, like the one most of us have in our pockets, that don\u2019t just do one one human understands faster, but that can take the collective efforts of millions of humans over decades to make it possible for us to play \u201cAngry Birds\u201d.\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-14 medium-7\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide09.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide09.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n</div>\n<p>\n</p><center>\nThis is an amazing accomplishment for our species, but AI goes beyond it.\n</center>\n<p></p>\n<center>\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide11.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide11.png\" width=\"75%\"></a>\n  <br><br>\n</center>\n<p>\nAll of the previous capabilities can only solve problems humans already understand well enough to solve.\n</p>\n<p>\nAI allows us to solve problems no humans understand.\n</p>\n<p>\nI\u2019m going to focus now on Machine Learning, which is just one sub-field of AI, but it\u2019s the one that has the most hype over the past decade, and one that is being rapidly deployed in ways that automate human tasks.\n</p>\n<p>\nThe main distinction between traditional computing and machine learning is that traditional automatons are programmed, while machine learning automatons are trained. our brains and the\nexperiences we relate to are linear \u2013 we get one year older every\nyear, and can relate to linear change easily.   \n<p></p>\n<p>\nBut, nearly everything we care about is actually changes exponentially.\n</p>\n</div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-14 medium-7\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide28.png\"><img the way nearly everything that matters\nchanges.\n<p></p><p>\nAll it means to be <em>exponential</em>, is that the change is a\npercentage of the current value, like increasing by 5% a year or\ndoubling every decade.\n\n</p>\n  </div>\n\n<p></p></div><p></p>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide29.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide29.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\nHere\u2019s what doubling every year looks like for 10 years (compared to the blue line which is adding 100 every year).\nLooks about the same, until we go out another year...\n  </div>\n\n<p></p></div><p></p>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-8 medium-4\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide30.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide30.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-8 medium-4\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide31.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide31.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-8 medium-4\">\n  <a medium-4\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide33.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide33.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-8 medium-4\">\n  <a medium-4\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide36.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide36.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-8 medium-4\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide37.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide37.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-8 medium-4\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide38.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide38.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-16 medium-8\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide39.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide39.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-8 medium-4\">\n<p><br></p>\n<p>\nAfter 20 years, the linear line is so overwhelmed by the exponential\ngrowth, that it is indistinguishable from the flat axis. In another\n10 years, the doubling rate has produced another factor of 1000.\n</p>\n  </div>\n\n<p></p></div><p></p>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-16 medium-8\">\n  <a href=\"https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/maddison-data-gdp-per-capita-in-2011us-single-benchmark\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/gdpgrowth.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-8 medium-4\">\n<p><br></p>\n<p>\nAlmost everything we care about looks like this \u2013 here we see GDP per capita for a handful of countries.\n</p>\n<p>\nThe exponential growth is so powerful, that World War II looks like a little glitch.\n</p>\n  </div>\n\n<p></p></div>\n<br>\n<p>\nWe see similar exponential curves for almost anything we look at \u2013 here it is books published per person, and crop yield per acre.\n</p><p></p>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/new-books-per-million.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/new-books-per-million.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/long-term-cereal-yields-in-the-united-kingdom.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/long-term-cereal-yields-in-the-united-kingdom.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<p></p><p>\nPredicting exponential growth is the easiest and safest prediction\nto make. The only way it goes wrong is if there is some physical limit\nthat stops the growth \u2013 Malthus thought we were at the limit of how\nmuch an acre of land could produce in 1800 \u2013 but you can see from the\ngraph on the right that we weren\u2019t, and this is one of the reasons he\ngot things so wrong.  </p><p></p>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-14 medium-7\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide42.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide42.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-10 medium-5\">\n<p><br></p>\n<p>\nThe main challenge isn\u2019t predicting the exponential growth, it is guess how much more growth is needed for things to work \u2013 that is, what are the labels on the vertical axis.\n\n</p>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-14 medium-7\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide43.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide43.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-10 medium-5\">\n<p><br></p>\n<p>\nOne property of exponential curves, is if you zoom in on any part of them, they look basically the same.\n</p>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-14 medium-7\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide44.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide44.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-10 medium-5\">\n<p><br></p>\n<p>\nWhen autonomous vehicles started to work in 2005, if you thought we\nwere on a curve like this and only needed to get about 100<em>x</em> better,\nyou were at risk of making really bad predictions.\n</p>\n<div class=\"midcaption\">\nImage: <a href=\"https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:DARPAGrandChallenge2005-StanleyAward.jpg\">Stanley winning DARPA Grand Challenge 2005</a>\n</div>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-14 medium-7\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide45.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide45.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-10 medium-5\">\n\nIf you saw the rate of improvement in autonomous vehicles from 2004 to\n2016 and extrapolated continued exponential growth, maybe it wasn't so\nunreasonable to predict in 2016 that we would have coast-to-coast full\nautonomy by 2017.\n\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-14 medium-7\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide46.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide46.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-10 medium-5\">\n<p>\nOf course, no we know it was a lot further off.\n</p>\n<p>\n\nEven though its easy to predict exponential change, it is hard to\npredict looking forward how much more change is needed to get to the\npoint where something actually works well enough.\n\n</p>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-10 medium-5\">\n<p>\n<br>\n</p>\n<p>\nPredicting dropping costs is easier. The red curve is also exponential, but here, it is decreasing by 7% a year instead of increasing.\n</p>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-14 medium-7\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide47.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide47.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide48.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide48.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p>\nFor example, let\u2019s look at the cost of communicating.\n</p>\n<p>\nIf you wanted to send a message from Washington to California in 1800, you couldn\u2019t even imagine doing it.\n</p>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide49.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide49.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p>\nBy 1803, one person could \u2013 Thomas Jefferson envisioned the Lewis and Clark expedition.\n</p>\n<p>\nIt cost <span>$</span>50,000 in 1803 dollars, which is hard to convert, but let\u2019s guess about <span>$</span>100M. </p><p class=\"aside\">Congressional appropriation methods, however, haven't changed much - the initial request, shown in the letter, was for $2,500.</p>\n<p></p>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide51.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide51.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p>\nLet\u2019s zoom in on the next 100 years.\n</p>\n<p>\nBy 1903, we had telephones. If you were really rich and important you could have one in your office.\n</p>\n<p>\nA 3-minute call from NY to Chicago cost <span>$</span>5.45 in 1903 dollars. </p><p class=\"aside\">I couldn\u2019t find out if you could call from Washington to California, and adjusting for inflation is tough, but somewhere around a few $1000 seems reasonable.</p>\n<p></p>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<p></p><center>\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide52.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide52.png\" width=\"80%\"></a>\n</center>\n<br>\n<p>\nWhy was it so expensive? Here\u2019s the phone operators that were needed\nto make it happen. (Those jobs don\u2019t exist today either.)\n</p><p></p>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide53.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide53.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p>\n\nBy 2003, we had early smartphones.\n</p>\n<p>\nToday, no one thinks twice about the cost of sending messages to\nCalifornia.\n</p>\n<p>\nSending messages has gotten so ridiculously cheap, spam is profitable.\n</p>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide54.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide54.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p>\nOne way to think about the jobs of the future is to predict when the\ncost of automating the job will drop below the cost of hiring a human\nto do it today.\n</p>\n<p>\nNearly all individual jobs could be automated today, if you invested\nenough in a special-purpose solution and in training the machine to do\nthat specific job.\n\n</p>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide55.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide55.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide57.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide57.png\"></a>\n  </div>\n</div>\n\n<p></p><center><p></p>\n\n<h2 id=\"jobs-that-won-t-be-replaced-by-ai\">Jobs that Won\u2019t be Replaced by AI</h2>\n\n<p></p></center>\n<p>\nIt is hard to predict <em>when</em> particular jobs will be automated out of\nexistence, but it seems eventually most jobs people have today will\nbe.\n</p>\n<p>\nI think there are three types of jobs that won\u2019t be \u2013 the first two\nwill be a bit depressing, but don\u2019t give up hope for a hopeful\nconclusion until I get to the third one.\n</p><p></p>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide59.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide59.png\"></a><br>\n<div class=\"midcaption\"><center>Image: \n<a href=\"https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:US_Navy_050419-N-6665R-030_Dr._Akshay_Dalal_provides_medication_for_an_Indonesian_patient_as_the_medical_team_aboard_the_Military_Sealift_Command_(MSC)_hospital_ship_USNS_Mercy_(T-AH_19)_performs_surgery.jpg\">US Navy</a><br>\n<a href=\"https://www.wsj.com/articles/robots-vs-anesthesiologists-1380150756\"><em>Robots vs. Aneasthesiologists</em></a><br>\n<a over for the robot intended to replace anesthesiologists</em></a>\n</center>\n</div>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n\nAn example of a profession that is well organized to resist job losses\ndue to technology is <a href=\"https://www.nytimes.com/2019/09/13/podcasts/1619-slavery-healthcare.html?showTranscript=1\">medicine</a>. When\nJohnson &amp; Johnson marketed a machine for performing anesthesia,\nthe American Society of Anesthesiologists <a href=\"https://www.technologyreview.com/s/601141/automated-anesthesiologist-suffers-a-painful-defeat/\">objected</a>. \n<p></p>\n<p>\nThe\nmedical professional organizations are so powerful, that\nnearly all medical technology is designed and marketed to assist\ndoctors, not replace them. \n</p>\n  </div>\n\n<p></p></div><p></p>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p><br></p>\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide60.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide60.png\"></a><br>\n<div class=\"midcaption\"><center>\nAmazon Fulfillment Center, Richmond, VA<br> (picture by me)\n</center>\n</div>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p>\n\nIt is well worth taking a tour of an Amazon Fulfillment Center. Other\nthan conveyer belts, scanners, and fork lifts, there are not many many\nmachines operating the warehouse \u2014 nearly all the work is done\nby humans, being told by software what to do. </p>\n<p>\nIts cheaper to get\nhumans to do this work since it is physically complex, varied, and\nhigh skill, but the skills required to do it are readily available and\nnot highly valued by the marketplace, so humans with such skills can\nbe hired at low cost and easily replaced when necessary. It'll be a\nlong time before it is cheaper to do these jobs by machine.\n  </p></div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p><br></p>\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide61.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide61.png\"></a><br>\n<div class=\"midcaption\"><center>\n\n</center>\n</div>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p>\nWe don\u2019t watch sports to see the fastest, strongest, most agile\nobjects in the universe - we want to be inspired by the individual\nachievements of other humans, and the synergy of a team of humans\nworking together.\n</p>\n<p>\n\nUnfortunately for me, and most of us in this room (unlike Dexter Manley,\npictured, who was our keynote speaker), we don\u2019t have the\nathletic talents to succeed as professional athletes.\n\n</p>\n<p>\nBut this intrinsic value in work being done by humans applies to many other endeavors.\n</p>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide62.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide62.png\"></a><br>\n<div class=\"midcaption\"><center>\n<a href=\"https://pixabay.com/photos/music-concert-monkey-guitar-stage-3507317/\">Image Credit: Papafox</a>\n</center>\n</div>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p>\n\nMachines can already produce music that musicologists can\u2019t\ndistinguish from humans \u2014 but no one wants to listen to it.\n</p>\n<p>\nWe want to listen to music to connect with the humans (or at least\nanimals!) who wrote and performed it, and it matters who they are.\n</p>\n</div></div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide63.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide63.png\"></a><br>\n<div class=\"midcaption\"><center>\nImage Credit: <a href=\"https://pixabay.com/photos/chef-cook-kitchen-cooking-food-1903882/\">hellotimothytyndale</a>\n</center>\n</div>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p>\nBurger flippers can be replaced by machines once the costs get low\nenough, but not high-end chefs \u2014 food tastes more savory when we\ncan imagine it being cooked by a perfectionist chef lording over a\nkitchen.\n</p>\n</div>\n</div>\n\n<div class=\"row\" style=\"margin-top:10px\">\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide64.png\"><img src=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide64.png\"></a><br>\n<div class=\"midcaption\"><center>\nWhite House Photo (Public Domain)\n</center>\n</div>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"column small-12 medium-6\">\n<p>\nEducation works best when the teacher is a human who cares about what she is teaching, and best of all when the teacher also cares about the students as people.\n</p>\n</div>\n</div>\n<center>\n## Hopeful Conclusion\n</center>\n<p>\nIn the future, we should <em>all</em> have jobs like these!\n</p>\n<p>\nEveryone should have a job that values their intrinsic humanity, and\ntechnology will soon advance to the point where we all can. In many\nways (recall the diminishing percentage of our workforce employed in\nagriculture from earlier) we already have. We just have\nto face the challenge of restructuring society to make this work.\n\n</p>\n<center>\n  <a href=\"/images/jobsforhumans/Slide66.png\"><img width=\"70%\"
