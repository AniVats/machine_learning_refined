University</strong>\u2014<strong>Early Life Project, 1819-1870</strong> <strong>(JUEL)</strong>,&nbsp;a place to encounter what life was like in the first years of the University of Virginia. Jefferson\u2019s vision of a secular university, dedicated to enriching public life and sustaining the new republic, was both embodied in and transformed by the people who lived, worked, and studied at the University. Bringing together a trove of personal and administrative documents, as well as archival images of the university and three-dimensional digital renderings, JUEL invites users to discover the people and places of the University\u2019s early years, stretching from its founding in 1819 through the end of the Civil War.</p>\n<p style=\"text-align: justify;\">JUEL was cofounded by <a href=\"http://provost.utexas.edu/maurie-mcinnis\" title=\"MAURIE MCINNIS\">Maurie McInnis</a>, former University of Virginia Professor of Art History and Vice Provost for Academic Affairs (presently at University of Texas at Austin), and <a href=\"http://college.as.virginia.edu/people/kv2h\" title=\"KIRT VON DAACKE\">Kirt von Daacke</a>, Associate Professor and Dean, Department of History. The current Principal Investigators of JUEL are Professors Kirt von Daacke and <a href=\"http://www.arch.virginia.edu/faculty/Louis_Nelson\" title=\"LEWIS NELSON\">Louis P. Nelson</a>&nbsp;(School of Architecture).
