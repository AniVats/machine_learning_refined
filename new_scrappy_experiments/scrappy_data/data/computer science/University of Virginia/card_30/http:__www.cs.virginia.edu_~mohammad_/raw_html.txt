In particular, I am interested in  <em>foundations of cryptography</em> and its interplay with computational complexity. I am also interested in <em>adversarial machine learning</em> from a formal perspective. In both of these areas, I am particularly interested in understanding inherent <em>barriers</em> (aka \"lower bounds\", \"impossibility results\", or \"separations\") that might exist against using computational intractability assumptions. \n\n<br>\n\n\n</p><p> I received my PhD from the <a href=\"http://www.cs.princeton.edu\">Computer Science Department</a> of <a href=\"http://www.princeton.edu\">Princeton University</a> under the supervision of <a href=\"https://www.boazbarak.org/\">Boaz Barak</a>. Prior to joining  UVA, I was a postdoctoral research associate in the <a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu\">Computer Science Department</a> of <a href=\"http://www.cornell.edu\">Cornell University</a> under the supervision of  <a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu/~rafael/\">Rafael Pass</a>.\n\n\n<br>\n\n</p><p> For the 2019-2020 academic year, I am on sabbatical leave visiting  <a
