Cheatham Professor of\nSciences at the Computer Science Department at the University of\nVirginia. From 2004 to 2012, she served as the Department Chair at\nUVA.&nbsp; From 1977 to 2004, she was a Professor of Computer Science\nat the University of Pittsburgh and also served as the Dean of Graduate\nStudies in the College of Arts and Sciences from 1991 to 1996. Her\nresearch interests include optimizing compilers, virtual execution\nenvironments, software testing, program analysis, software\nsecurity,&nbsp; and software systems for multi-core\narchitectures.&nbsp; She has published over 175 articles and&nbsp; has\ndirected 32 Ph.D. students to completion, half of whom are women.</p>\n<p>Mary Lou received the Distinguished Alumni Award at the 50th\nCelebration of the Department of Computer Science, University of\nPittsburgh in 2017. &nbsp;She was awarded the IEEE 2015 TCSE Software\nEngineering Women in Science and Engineering Leadership Award\n(WISE).&nbsp; She received the&nbsp; SIGSOFT Influential Educator Award\nin 2014 and the&nbsp; Ken Kennedy Award in 2012 for contributions to\ncompiler technology and software engineering, exemplary service to the\nprofession, and lifelong dedication to mentoring and improving\ndiversity in computing.&nbsp; Mary Lou is both&nbsp; an IEEE Fellow\nand&nbsp; an ACM Fellow. She&nbsp; received the Anita Borg Technical\nLeadership Award in 2011 and was selected for the White House\u2019s\nPresidential Award for Excellence in Science, Mathematics and\nEngineering Mentoring the same year. She received the Computing\nResearch Association (CRA) Nico Habermann Award in 2006&nbsp; and was\nselected as a Girl Scout Woman of Distinction in 2003 .
