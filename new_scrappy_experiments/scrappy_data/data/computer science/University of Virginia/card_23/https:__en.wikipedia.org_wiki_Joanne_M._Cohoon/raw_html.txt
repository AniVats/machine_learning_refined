href=\"#External_links\"><span class=\"tocnumber\">5</span> <span class=\"toctext\">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Biography\">Biography</span><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a href=\"/w/index.php?title=Joanne_M._Cohoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1\" title=\"Edit section: Biography\">edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Cohoon received a <a href=\"/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts\" title=\"Bachelor of Arts\">B.A.</a> in <a href=\"/wiki/Philosophy\" title=\"Philosophy\">Philosophy</a> from <a href=\"/wiki/Ramapo_College\" title=\"Ramapo College\">Ramapo College</a> in 1976. She received a <a href=\"/wiki/Master_of_Arts\" title=\"Master of Arts\">M.A.</a> in Student Personnel Administration in Higher Education from <a href=\"/wiki/Columbia_University\" title=\"Columbia University\">Columbia University</a> Teachers College in 1979 and a <a href=\"/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy\" title=\"Doctor of Philosophy\">Ph.D</a> in <a href=\"/wiki/Sociology\" title=\"Sociology\">Sociology</a> from the <a href=\"/wiki/University_of_Virginia\" title=\"University of Virginia\">University of Virginia</a> in 2000.\n</p><p>She was a Research Assistant Professor at the <a href=\"/wiki/University_of_Virginia\" title=\"University of Virginia\">University of Virginia</a> from 2000 to 2003. From 2004 to 2016 she was a Senior Research Scientist with the National Center for Women in Information Technology (NCWIT). In 2005 she became an Adjunct Research Professor in the Department of Sociology at the  <a href=\"/wiki/University_of_Virginia\" title=\"University of Virginia\">University of Virginia</a>, and in 2010 she was an Associate Professor in the Department of Science, Technology and Society, also at the <a href=\"/wiki/University_of_Virginia\" title=\"University of Virginia\">University of Virginia</a>. In 2016 Cohoon was promoted to Professor.\n</p><p>Cohoon, who had metastatic breast cancer, died on February 14, 2016 at the age of 61.<sup id=\"cite_ref-1\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-1\">[1]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Career\">Career</span><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a href=\"/w/index.php?title=Joanne_M._Cohoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2\" title=\"Edit section: Career\">edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Cohoon researched the gender imbalance in computing, and focused on putting this knowledge into practice. She wrote a paper showing evidence that culture and social structures contribute to fewer women in the field of computing technology.<sup id=\"cite_ref-2\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-2\">[2]</a></sup> At NCWIT she was a key senior research scientist and the co-founder of Extension Services for Undergraduate Programs (ES-UP),<sup id=\"cite_ref-3\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-3\">[3]</a></sup> a nationwide program that provides customized consultation to undergraduate computing programs to help them increasing women\u2019s participation. She was also active in the Pacesetters program: a fast-track program where committed corporate, entrepreneurial, and academic leaders work across organizational boundaries to accelerate change that improves the meaningful participation of technical women in their organizations.<sup id=\"cite_ref-4\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-4\">[4]</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Awards\">Awards</span><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a href=\"/w/index.php?title=Joanne_M._Cohoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3\" title=\"Edit section: Awards\">edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 2015 she won the A. Richard Newton Educator ABIE Award for her work in developing practices that increase women in computing.<sup id=\"cite_ref-5\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-5\">[5]</a></sup> Since her death, NCWIT has named a plenary at their annual Summit after Joanne.<sup id=\"cite_ref-6\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-6\">[6]</a></sup> At this plenary, leading social science researchers speak on wide-ranging and thought-provoking topics related to women's participation in computing and technology.\n</p>\n<h2><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"References\">References</span><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a href=\"/w/index.php?title=Joanne_M._Cohoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4\" title=\"Edit section: References\">edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class=\"reflist\" style=\"list-style-type: decimal;\">\n<div class=\"mw-references-wrap\"><ol class=\"references\">\n<li id=\"cite_note-1\"><span class=\"mw-cite-backlink\"><b><a href=\"#cite_ref-1\">^</a></b></span> <span class=\"reference-text\"><cite class=\"citation web\">The Daily Progress (2016-02-17). <a rel=\"nofollow\" class=\"external text\" href=\"http://www.dailyprogress.com/obituaries/cohoon-joanne-louise-mcgrath/article_180206e6-5768-5587-9c85-c455f593bd2f.html\">\"Obituary, Cohoon, Joanne Louise McGrath\"</a>.
