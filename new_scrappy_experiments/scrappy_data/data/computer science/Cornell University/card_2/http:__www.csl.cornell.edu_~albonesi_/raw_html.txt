Albonesi</b><br>\n        Professor<br>\n\t</h2>\n\n<p>\n<a href=\"http://www.csl.cornell.edu/\">Computer Systems Laboratory</a><br>\n<a href=\"http://www.ece.cornell.edu/\">School of Electrical and Computer Engineering</a><br>\n<a href=\"http://www.cornell.edu\">Cornell University</a><br> \n333 <a href=\"rhodes.html\">Rhodes Hall</a><br> \n<a href=\"http://www.visitithaca.com\">Ithaca</a>, NY 14853<br> \n(607) 254-5473<br>\ndha7 at cornell.edu\n</p><p>\n\n</p><p>\n\nI joined the <a href=\"http://www.csl.cornell.edu\">Computer Systems\nLaboratory</a> in 2004 after eight years at the <a href=\"http://www.rochester.edu\">University of Rochester</a> Before returning to graduate school to pursue an academic career, I spent ten years in the computer industry as a technical manager, lead architect, and chip designer.\n\n</p><p>\n\nMy primary research focus for the last 20+ years is power-efficient computer architecture. Recent research contributions include scalable power management algorithms, dynamically adaptive computer architectures, accelerator architectures for deep neural networks, dynamic GPGPU power management, shared reconfigurable architectures for chip multiprocessors, architectures for accelerating human-computer interaction, and applications to energy-efficient smart buildings. \n\n</p><p>\n\nI am a <a href=\"https://www.ieee.org/membership/fellows/index.html\">Fellow</a>\nof the IEEE, and received the <a href=\"https://www.nsf.gov/funding/pgm_summ.jsp?pims_id=503214\">National Science Foundation\nCAREER Award</a>, three IEEE Micro <a href=\"https://www.computer.org/digital-library/magazines/mi/call-for-papers-special-issue-on-top-picks-from-the-2019-computer-architecture-conferences\">Top Picks Awards</a>, three\n<a href=\"https://www.research.ibm.com/university/awards/faculty.html\">IBM\nFaculty Awards</a>, and the <a href=\"https://www.microarch.org/tot/\">International Symposium on Microarchitecture Test of Time Award</a>. I was General co-Chair of the <a href=\"http://www.microarch.org/micro42\">42nd International\nSymposium on Microarchitecture</a> and Program Chair of the  <a href=\"http://www.ece.cmu.edu/calcm/isca2015\">42nd International\nSymposium on Computer Architecture</a>. Currently I serve on the Editorial Board of <a href=\"https://www.computer.org/csdl/magazine/co\">IEEE\nComputer</a>; past editorial service includes Editor-in-Chief of <a href=\"http://www.computer.org/micro\">IEEE Micro</a>. \n\n</p><p>\n\nIn terms of teaching, I have received the Kenneth A. Goldman '71,\nMichael Tien '72, and Ralph S. Watts '72 <a href=\"https://www.engineering.cornell.edu/research-and-faculty/faculty/resources-faculty/faculty-teaching-and-advising-award-winners\">College\nof Engineering Excellence in Teaching Awards</a>; and the <a href=\"https://www.ece.cornell.edu/ece/people/faculty-awards-and-honors/ruth-and-joel-spira-award-excellence-teaching\">Ruth\nand Joel Spira Excellence in ECE Teaching Award</a> (twice). In 2015,\nI created a MOOC, <a href=\"https://www.edx.org/course/computing-technology-inside-smartphone-cornellx-engri1210x\">The\nComputing Technology Inside Your Smartphone</a>, from a freshmen\nclass.  Subsequently, I turned that <a href=\"https://classes.cornell.edu/browse/roster/SP20/class/ENGRI/1210\">freshmen\nclass</a> into a <a href=\"https://teaching.cornell.edu/teaching-resources/designing-your-course/flipping-classroom\">flipped\nclassroom</a>.
