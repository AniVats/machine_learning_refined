Constable</b> \nheads the Nuprl research group in automated reasoning and \nsoftware verification, and joined the Cornell faculty in 1968.  \nHe has supervised over forty PhD students in computer science, \nincluding the very first graduate of the CS department.  \nHe is known for his work connecting programs and mathematical proofs, \nwhich has led to new ways of automating the production of reliable software.  \nHe has written three books on this topic as well as numerous research articles.  \nProfessor Constable is a graduate of Princeton University where he worked with \nAlonzo Church, one of the pioneers of computer science.</p>\n\n<p>Professor Constable stepped down as the Dean of the Faculty of Computing and \nInformation Science at Cornell University, serving from 1999 - 2009.
