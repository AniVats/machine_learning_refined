We perform research in the broad area of <b><i>Network Systems Biology</i></b> with both high-throughput experimental (see <a href=\"./PDF/Vo_Cell2016.pdf\">Vo et al., <i>Cell</i> 2016</a>) and integrative computational (see <a href=\"./PDF/Chen_Nature_Genetics_2018.pdf\">Chen et al., <i>Nature Genetics</i> 2018</a>) methodologies, aiming to understand gene functions and their relationships within complex molecular networks and how perturbations to such systems may lead to various human diseases. The complexity of biological systems calls for building experimentally-verified computational models based on high-quality large-scale datasets, which is truly the future of biomedical research and the main theme of the lab. Our research is focused in five main areas:</p>\n\t\t\n\t\t<div id=\"text-left\"><h3>Functional and Comparative Genomics</h3>\n\t\t\tIntegrating information from DNA sequences, gene expression, protein structures and other functional genomics datasets to elucidate gene function, understand network topology and evolution, and ultimately to combine all of these insights and techniques to make accurate prognoses for cancer and other diseases.\n\t\t</div>\n\t\t<div id=\"pic-right\"><img src=\"images/graphics/genome.png\" width=\"100\" height=\"100\"></div>\n\t\t\n\t\t<div id=\"pic-left\" style=\"margin-top:35px\"><img src=\"images/graphics/network2.png\" width=\"100\" height=\"100\"></div>\n\t\t<div id=\"text-right\"><h3>Molecular and Dynamic Proteomics</h3>\n\t\t\tGenerating and analyzing genome-wide protein interactome (protein-protein and protein-DNA, in particular) maps for various organisms both computationally (with topological analysis and machine-learning approaches) and experimentally (with high-throughput Y2H, PCA, wNAPPA, and LUMIER assays); Investigating the dynamics of these networks upon perturbation (genetic variation, disease mutation, viral infection, environmental stress, etc). \n\t\t</div>\n\t\t\n\t\t<div id=\"text-left\"><h3>Structural Genomics and Simulations</h3>\n\t\t\tCombining three-dimensional protein structural information with current protein networks to better understand the role of each protein in the network, their relationships, and dynamics upon perturbation.\n\t\t</div>\n\t\t<div id=\"pic-right\"><img src=\"images/graphics/Structure.png\" width=\"100\" height=\"100\"></div>\n\t\t\n\t\t<div id=\"pic-left\"><img src=\"images/graphics/Topo.png\" width=\"100\" height=\"100\"></div>\n\t\t<div id=\"text-right\"><h3>Algorithms and Tools</h3>\n\t\t\tTo facilitate various research projects in the lab, we actively develop new algorithms and tools to analyze different genomic and proteomic datasets.
