Previously, Greg served as the Dean of <a href=\"http://cis.cornell.edu\">Computing and Information Sciences</a> (CIS)\n  at Cornell University, which houses the departments of <a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu\">Computer\n  Science</a>, <a href=\"http://www.infosci.cornell.edu\">Information\n  Science</a>, and <a href=\"http://www.stat.cornell.edu\">Statistical Sciences</a>.  Before this, \n  he held the Allen B. Cutting Chair in Computer Science at Harvard\n  University from 2004-2015.  At Harvard, he also served as the\n  Associate Dean for Computer Science and Electrical Engineering and\n  as the Director of the Center for Research on Computation and\n  Society.  Before Harvard, Morrisett spent eight years on the faculty\n  of Cornell's Computer Science Department.  He received his\n  bachelor's degree from the University of Richmond and both his\n  Master's and Doctorate degrees from Carnegie Mellon University.\n  </p>\n<p>\n  Professor Morrisett's research focuses on the application of\n  programming language technology for building secure, reliable, and\n  high-performance software systems.  A common theme is the focus on\n  systems-level languages and tools that can help detect or prevent\n  common vulnerabilities in software.  Past examples include typed\n  assembly language, proof-carrying code, software fault isolation,\n  and control-flow isolation.  Recently, his research focuses on\n  building provably correct and secure software, including a focus on\n  cryptographic schemes, machine learning, and compilers.\n</p>\n<p>\n  Morrisett is a Fellow of the ACM and has received a number of awards\n  for his research on programming languages, type systems, and\n  software security, including a Presidential Early Career Award for\n  Scientists and Engineers, an IBM Faculty Fellowship, an NSF Career\n  Award, and an Alfred P. Sloan Fellowship.\n</p>\n<p>\nHe served as Chief Editor for the <i>Journal of Functional\nProgramming</i> and as an associate editor for <i>ACM Transactions on\nProgramming Languages and Systems</i>, <i>Information Processing\n  Letters</i>, and <i>The Journal of the ACM</i>. He currently serves\n  as co-editor-in-chief for the <i>Research Highlights</i> column of\n<i>Communications of the ACM</i>.  In\naddition, Morrisett has served on the DARPA Information Science and\nTechnology Study (ISAT) Group, the NSF Computer and Information\nScience and Engineering (CISE) Advisory Council, The Max Planck\nInstitute for Software Systems Advisory Board, the Computing Research\nAssociation Board, Microsoft Research's\nTechnical Advisory Board, Microsoft's\nTrusthworthy Computing Academic Advisory Board, and the Fortify\nTechnical Advisory Board.\n</p>\n</blockquote>\t\n</blockquote>\t\n<hr style=\"height: 2px; width: 100%;\">\n\n<br>\n\n<h2>Some Previous Research Projects</h2>\n\n<table style=\"text-align:\" border=\"0\" cellpadding=\"2\" cellspacing=\"2\">\n <tbody>\n    <tr>\n     <td style=\"vertical-align: top;\">\n<a title=\"By Remi Jouan (Photo taken by Remi Jouan) [GFDL (http://www.gnu.org/copyleft/fdl.html), CC-BY-SA-3.0 (http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/) or CC BY-SA 2.5-2.0-1.0 (http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/2.5-2.0-1.0)], via Wikimedia Commons\" href=\"http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File%3ACoq_Belle-Ile.jpg\"><img width=\"55px\" alt=\"Coq Belle-Ile\" src=\"//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Coq_Belle-Ile.jpg/512px-Coq_Belle-Ile.jpg\"></a>\n\n     </td>\n     <td style=\"vertical-align: top;\">\n       <a href=\"http://www.cs.princeton.edu/~appel/certicoq/\"><b>CertiCoq:</b></a> \n      <span class=\"project\">\n       The CertiCoq project aims to build a proven-correct compiler for dependently-typed, functional languages, such as Gallina--the core language of the Coq proof assistant. Our tools do not require any \n        integration with the optimizer to work, and can also be used on \n        hand-optimized code.
