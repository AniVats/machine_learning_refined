Hopcroft</b> is the IBM Professor of Engineering and Applied \nMathematics in <a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu/\">Computer Science</a> at \nCornell University. From January 1994 until June 2001, he was the Joseph \nSilbert Dean of Engineering. After receiving both his M.S. (1962) and Ph.D. \n(1964) in electrical engineering from <a href=\"http://www.stanford.edu/\">Stanford University</a>, he spent three years on \nthe faculty of <a href=\"http://www.princeton.edu/main/\">Princeton \nUniversity</a>. He joined the Cornell faculty in 1967, was named professor in \n1972 and the Joseph C. Ford Professor of Computer Science in 1985. He served as \nchairman of the Department of Computer Science from 1987 to 1992 and was the \nassociate dean for college affairs in 1993. An undergraduate alumnus of <a href=\"http://www.seattleu.edu/\">Seattle University</a>, Hopcroft was honored \nwith a Doctor of Humanities Degree, Honoris Causa, in 1990.</p>\n<p>Hopcroft's research centers on theoretical aspects of computing, especially \nanalysis of algorithms, automata theory, and graph algorithms. He has coauthored \nfour books on formal languages and algorithms with <a href=\"http://infolab.stanford.edu/~ullman/\">Jeffrey D. Ullman</a> and <a href=\"http://www1.cs.columbia.edu/~aho/\">Alfred V. Aho.</a> His most recent work \nis on the study of information capture and access.</p>\n<p>He was honored with the <a href=\"http://awards.acm.org/homepage.cfm?srt=all&amp;awd=140/\">A. M. Turing \nAward</a> in 1986. He is a member of the National Academy of Sciences (<a href=\"http://www.nasonline.org/site/PageServer\">NAS</a>), \nthe National Academy of Engineering <a href=\"http://www.nae.edu/nae/naehome.nsf\">(NAE),</a> a foreign member of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, and a fellow of the American \nAcademy of Arts and Sciences<a href=\"http://www.amacad.org/\"> (AAAS)</a>, the \nAmerican Association for the Advancement of Science, the Institute of Electrical \nand Electronics Engineers <a href=\"http://www.ieee.org/portal/site\">(IEEE)</a>, \nand the Association of Computing Machinery <a href=\"http://www.acm.org/\">(ACM)</a>. In 1992, he was appointed by President \nBush to the National Science Board <a href=\"http://www.nsf.gov/nsb/\">(NSB)</a>, \nwhich oversees the National Science Foundation <a href=\"http://www.nsf.gov/\">(NSF)</a>, and served through May 1998. From 1995-98, \nHopcroft served on the National Research Council's Commission on Physical \nSciences, Mathematics, and Applications. </p>\n<p>In addition to these appointments, Hopcroft serves as a member of the <a href=\"http://www.siam.org/\">SIAM</a> \nfinancial management committee, <a href=\"http://www.iiitd.ac.in/\">IIIT New Delhi</a> \nadvisory board, <a href=\"http://www.microsoft.com/asia/\">Microsoft</a>'s \ntechnical advisory board for research Asia, and the Engineering Advisory Board,\n<a href=\"http://www.seattleu.edu/\">Seattle University</a>.<br></p>\n\n\n<h2>Courses</h2>\n<ul class=\"bridge-links\">\n\n <li><a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu/Courses/cs381/2007fa/\"> \n\t<b>CS 381</b> -
