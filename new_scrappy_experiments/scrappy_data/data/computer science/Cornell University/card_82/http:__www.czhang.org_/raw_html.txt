His research interests are ubiquitous computing and human-computer interaction(HCI). More specifically,&nbsp; his&nbsp;research examines how to solicit information on and around the human body to address real-world challenges in various application areas, such as <span style=\"color: #993300;\"><strong>interaction, health sensing, and activity recognition</strong></span>. <strong>&nbsp;</strong>His Ph.D. thesis presents over 10 different novel input techniques for wearables, some leveraging commodity devices while others incorporate new hardware. His work also has attracted the attention of various media outlets, including ScienceDaily, DigitalTrends, ZDNet, New Scientist, RT, TechRadar, Phys.org, Yahoo News, Business Insider, and MSN News.</p>\n<p style=\"text-align: left;\">He is also&nbsp;an alumnus of <a href=\"https://www.scheller.gatech.edu/centers-initiatives/tiger/index.html\">The Technological Innovation: Generating Economic Results&nbsp;</a>&nbsp;(TI:GER) program, which is a joint program held between the Business School at Georgia Tech and the Law School at Emory University. &nbsp;This program taught him how to commercialize new technology and present innovative products to the market.&nbsp;The work that leverages commodity devices has resulted in commercial impact, including licensing the watch input technology to Canadian startup <a href=\"http://www.proximityhci.com/\">ProximityHCI</a> to improve the smartwatch interaction experience.</p>\n<p style=\"text-align: left;\">Before coming to Georgia Tech, he received his bachelor\u2019s degree in software engineering from&nbsp;<a href=\"http://web.archive.org/web/20140518081113/http://www.nankai.edu.cn/english/\">Nankai University</a>&nbsp;and his master\u2019s degree from the&nbsp;<a href=\"http://web.archive.org/web/20140518081113/http://english.is.cas.cn/\">Institute of Software</a>&nbsp;at the&nbsp;<a href=\"http://web.archive.org/web/20140518081113/http://english.cas.cn/\">Chinese Academy of Sciences</a>&nbsp;as an outstanding graduate, where he started his&nbsp;curiosity&nbsp;on HCI and &nbsp;acquired his first-hand research experience across tangible user interface, HCI for children, music interface.</p>\n<p style=\"text-align: left;\">He is an enthusiastic&nbsp;sports fan and enjoys running in spare time.&nbsp;
