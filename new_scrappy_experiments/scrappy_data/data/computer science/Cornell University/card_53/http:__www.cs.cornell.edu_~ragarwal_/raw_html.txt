class=\"bottom-three\"><b>Networks that never drop packets</b>: One of the core problems of current network fabrics is packet drops, that directly or indirectly lead to inefficiency and complexity (e.g., high CPU utilization, high latency, complex network stacks). We are designing and building network fabrics and stacks that guarantee that packets will never be dropped. </li>\n\t  \n\t  <li class=\"bottom-three\"><b>Resource Disaggregation</b>: Shared-nothing architectures provide good data locality and cross-job isolation. However, for modern workloads where peak resource demands can be much higher than the average, shared-nothing architectures beget extreme resource underutilization, high cost and inflexibility. Disaggregating compute from storage has the potential to overcome these limitations! To realize this goal, we are working along several directions: \t\t  \n\t\t  \t<ul>\n\t\t  \t  <li>Network Fabric and Stacks [<a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu/~ragarwal/pubs/disaggregation.pdf\">OSDI'16</a>] [<a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu/~ragarwal/pubs/shoal.pdf\">NSDI'19</a>]\n\t\t\t  </li><li>Storage Stack for Disaggregated Architectures [<a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu/~ragarwal/pubs/i10.pdf\">NSDI'20</a>] \n\t\t  \t  </li><li>Efficient and Secure Query Execution on Disaggregated Storage [<a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu/~ragarwal/pubs/obladi.pdf\">OSDI'18</a>] [<a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu/~ragarwal/pubs/snowset.pdf\">NSDI'20</a>] \n\t\t  \t </li></ul> \n\t\t\t <b><font color=\"#3CB371\">$3M award from NSF, Google faculty research award, Open-sourced, Deployed in the real world.</font></b></li>\n\n\t  <li class=\"bottom-three\"><b>Near-Perfect Datacenter Transport Design</b>: We are designing and building datacenter transport designs that provide provable worst-case guarantees. Some of the projects include:\n\t\t  \t<ul>\n\t\t\t  <li>Sincronia [<a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu/~ragarwal/pubs/sincronia.pdf\">SIGCOMM'18</a>] for Coflows;\n\t\t  \t  </li><li>Universal Packet Scheduling [<a href=\"http://www.cs.cornell.edu/~ragarwal/pubs/ups.pdf\">NSDI'16</a>] for flexible packet scheduling;\n\t\t  \t </li></ul> \n\t\t\t <b><font color=\"#3CB371\">SIGCOMM'18 Best Student
