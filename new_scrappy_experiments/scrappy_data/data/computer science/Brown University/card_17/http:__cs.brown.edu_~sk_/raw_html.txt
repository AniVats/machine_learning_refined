<p>\n   Recently, I have decided to devote a substantial portion of my time\n   and energy to the hardest problem I've worked on: <strong>computer science\n   education</strong>. It's the hardest because it requires substantial work on\n   both technical and human-factors fronts; the audience is often\n   unsophisticated and vulnerable; and if you screw up, you can do real\n   damage to not only individuals but also the field and society. I\n   recently wrote up a manifesto for my new direction\n   [the same text is on both\n    <a href=\"https://www.facebook.com/shriram.krishnamurthi/posts/10157549593930581\">Facebook</a>\n    and\n    <a href=\"https://plus.google.com/+ShriramKrishnamurthi/posts/AF24ykaQYXw\">Google+</a>].\n </p>\n\n<p>\nSince 1995, decades before it became a fashionable hobby, I've been\ndevoted to computer science outreach at a national (and larger)\nscale. I also wrote the\nwidely-used\n<a href=\"http://www.plai.org/\">Programming Languages: Application and Interpretation</a>.\n</p>\n\n<p>\nThe current outreach program,\n<a href=\"http://www.bootstrapworld.org/\">Bootstrap</a>, is used\ninternationally for both \ncomputing and <em>math</em> education, and has been adopted as the middle-school mathematics curriculum \nby Code.org.
