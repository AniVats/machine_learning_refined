My research group is actively engaged in finding answers \nto fundamental questions in the broad areas listed below:\n\n<br> <br>\n<b> Smart and Connected Communities: </b> We are working on a range of problems\nin realizing the vision of connected and autonomus vehicles. GPS based\nlocation is at the heart of autonmous navigation. We are looking at new\ntechniques that allow vehicles to learn relative locations of other \nvehicles in the vicinity when GPS accuracies are low, such as in the downtown areas. \nWe are exploring new scalable techniques to communicate between vehicles and \nfrom vehicles to the infrastrcture so that we are ready to embrace the new\ngeneration of communication-capable smart-vehicles.\nMiniaturized sensor devices are going to increasingly permeate the world \naround us. Just in the last few years we have seen a rapid increase in the\nnumber of sensing modalities avaialble in our smartphones.\nSome are power hungry such as cameras, and some are inaccurate such as\naccelerometers. Many capabilities such as RFID scanning, RADAR and LIDAR and\neither large in physical dimensions or are expensive, but efforts are underway \nto miniaturize and integrate them into smartdevices. How can we efficiently\ncollect massive amounts of sensor data over wireless using such existing as \nwell as emerging capabilities?\nThrough this big data, how can we make sense of the physical world around us \nusing learning and deep data analysis tools to make more informed decisions \nfor a smarter and healthier life?
