Bio</h2>\n  <p>David Walker joined the department in 2002, was granted tenure in 2008 and&nbsp;was appointed a full professor in 2013. He received his doctoral and master\u2019s degrees in computer science from Cornell, and his bachelor\u2019s from Queen\u2019s University in Kingston, Ontario. &nbsp;During&nbsp;sabbaticals from Princeton, he has served as a visiting researcher at Microsoft Research in Redmond (2008) and in Cambridge (2009), and as Associate&nbsp;Visiting Faculty&nbsp;at the University of Pennsylvania (2015-2016).&nbsp;&nbsp;Professor Walker studies programming language theory, design and implementation, with an emphasis on the design of domain-specific languages. &nbsp;His awards include an NSF Career Award, a Sloan Fellowship and the 2015&nbsp;<span style=\"line-height: 18.9091px;\">ACM SIGPLAN Robin Milner Young Researcher Award.</span>&nbsp; Together with his collaborators, he&nbsp;has also won a&nbsp;<span style=\"line-height: 18.9091px;\">10-year retrospective award for the most influential paper at ACM POPL 1998, a</span>&nbsp;best paper award at ACM PLDI 2007, and a Community Award for his work&nbsp;at USENIX NSDI 2013. &nbsp;He served as an associate editor for ACM TOPLAS from&nbsp;2007-2015&nbsp;and as program chair for ACM POPL in 2015. &nbsp;</p>\n\n  <h2>\n    Selected Publications\n  </h2>\n  <ul>\n\t<li>\n\t\t<p><em>Don't Mind the Gap:&nbsp; Bridging Network-wide Objectives and Device-level Configurations</em>.&nbsp; Ryan Beckett, Ratul Mahajan, Todd Millstein, Jitu Padhye and David Walker.&nbsp; ACM SIGCOMM, August 2016.&nbsp; Winner of the&nbsp;SIGCOMM Best Paper Award.</p>\n\t</li>\n\t<li>\n\t\t<p>\u201c<em>Abstractions for Network Update</em>.\u201d&nbsp;Mark Reittblatt, Nate Foster, Jen Rexford, Cole Schlesinger and David Walker.
