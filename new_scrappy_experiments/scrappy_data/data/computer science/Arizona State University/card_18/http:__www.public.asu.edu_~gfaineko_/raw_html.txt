                    <b> Bio <a href=\"pub/fainekos_cv.pdf\" target=\"_blank\">[See also Curriculum Vitae]</a></b></h1>\n                        <p>\n                            Georgios Fainekos is an Associate Professor at the <a href=\"http://cidse.engineering.asu.edu/\" target=\"_blank\">School of Computing, Informatics and Decision Systems Engineering</a> at <a href=\"http://www.asu.edu/\" target=\"_blank\">Arizona State University</a>. \n                            He is director of the Cyber-Physical Systems Laboratory (CPSLab).\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\tHe is affiliated with the <a href=\"http://embedded.asu.edu/\" target=\"_blank\">Center for Embedded Systems (CES)</a> and he is also member of the <a href=\"https://robotics.asu.edu/\" target=\"_blank\">ASU Robotics</a> group.\n                            He received his Ph.D. in <a href=\"http://www.cis.upenn.edu\" target=\"_blank\">Computer and Information Science</a> from the <a href=\"http://www.upenn.edu\" target=\"_blank\">University of Pennsylvania</a> in 2008 where he was affiliated with the <a href=\"https://www.grasp.upenn.edu/\" target=\"_blank\">GRASP Lab</a>. \n                            Prof. Fainekos holds a Diploma degree (B.Sc. &amp; M.Sc.) in <a href=\"http://www.mech.ntua.gr/en\" target=\"_blank\">Mechanical Engineering</a> from the <a href=\"https://www.ntua.gr/en/\" target=\"_blank\">National Technical University of Athens</a> and an M.Sc. degree in <a href=\"http://www.cis.upenn.edu\" target=\"_blank\">Computer and Information Science</a> from the <a href=\"http://www.upenn.edu\" target=\"_blank\">University of Pennsylvania</a>. \n                            Before joining ASU, he held a Postdoctoral Researcher position at <a href=\"http://www.nec-labs.com/\" target=\"_blank\">NEC Laboratories America</a> in the System Analysis &amp; Verification Group. \n                            He is currently working in the area of Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS), autonomous systems, and mobile robots. \n                            In particular, his research interests include formal specification, modeling, testing, verification, synthesis, control, and monitoring for hybrid dynamical systems and discrete event systems.\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\tHis software toolbox, <a href=\"https://sites.google.com/a/asu.edu/s-taliro/\" target=\"_blank\">S-TaLiRo</a>, for requirements guided testing and monitoring of CPS has been nominated twice as a technological breakthrough by the industry. \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\tIn terms of automotive applications, Prof. Fainekos research interests span formal models, control, machine learning, and planning with ultimate goal of developing safe and dependable autonomous vehicles.\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\tProf. Fainekos is recipient of the <a href=\"http://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=1350420\" target=\"_blank\">NSF CAREER award</a>.\n                            He was also recipient of the SCIDSE Best Researcher Junior Faculty award for 2013 and of the <a href=\"http://sigbed.blogspot.com/p/awards.html\" target=\"_blank\">2008 Frank Anger Memorial ACM SIGBED/SIGSOFT Student Award</a>.\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<a href=\"pub/fainekos_cv.pdf\" target=\"_blank\"><b>[more ...]</b></a>\n                        </p>\n                        \n
