"<html><head><title> Computer Graphics Research at Caltech </title>\n\n</head><body background=\"http://www.cs.caltech.edu/pict/redpaper.jpg\">\n\n<center>\n<h1>  <font size=\"+4\">Computer Graphics Research at </font><a href=\"http://www.caltech.edu\"><font size=\"+4\">Caltech</font></a> </h1>\n\n<a href=\"Images/BI-i.gif\"><img src=\"Images/BI_small-i.gif\" height=\"138\"></a>\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<img src=\"Images/jorgen.gif\">\n&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href=\"Images/pblab.jpg\"><img src=\"Images/pblab.jpg\" height=\"138\"></a>\n\n</center>\n<p> \nGraphics-related research at Caltech primarily focuses on\nthe mathematical foundations of computer graphics.\nCaltech's research explores and develops\nnew approaches to modeling, rendering, simulation and scientific\nvisualization, and is highly connected to our work on\nhuman/computer interaction. New methods are needed to increase\nmodeling fidelity, ``fluency,'' and interactivity. This is\naccomplished using mathematical principles from differential\ngeometry, constrained optimization, integral equations and piecewise\ndifferential equations, as well as physical principles such as the\nmechanics of solids and the physics of light. The Caltech computer\ngraphics research efforts are led by\n<a href=\"~barr/index.html\">Alan Barr</a>,\n<a href=\"http://www.geometry.caltech.edu/~mathieu\">Mathieu Desbrun</a> (<a href=\"http://www.geometry.caltech.edu/\">Applied Geometry Lab</a>),\nand <a href=\"http://www.multires.caltech.edu/~ps\">Peter Schr\u00f6der</a>\n(Director, <a href=\"http://www.multires.caltech.edu/\">Multi-Res Modeling Group</a>).\nThe Caltech approach to computer graphics research is unique in its\nmathematical rigor. A common theme throughout our work is an emphasis\nboth on correct underlying mathematical foundations and on careful\nrealization in efficient, robust algorithms.\n</p><p>\n\n</p><hr>\n<p>\n\n</p><center>\n<h2> <a href=\"research_areas.html\"> <img src=\"Images/pandora.gif\" align=\"middle\">&nbsp;&nbsp; Research Areas &nbsp;&nbsp;<img
