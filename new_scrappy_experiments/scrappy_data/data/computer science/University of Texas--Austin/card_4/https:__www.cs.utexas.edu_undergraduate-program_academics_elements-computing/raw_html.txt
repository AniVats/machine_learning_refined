If a student still has questions after reading this website, they can email <a href=\"mailto:elements@cs.utexas.edu\" rel=\"nofollow\">elements@cs.utexas.edu</a> for further information and clarification.</p>\n<p><strong>Do I need to apply for the certificate to start taking courses?</strong></p>\n<p>Students must apply and have the certificate attached to their degree profile and set to pursuing&nbsp;in order to gain access to the upper division Elements electives. Students who do not have the certificate attached and set to pursuing will not be able to register for or waitlist the upper division&nbsp;courses. Students can apply for the certificate <a href=\"https://utdirect.utexas.edu/apps/sisutil/edoc/selection/\" rel=\"nofollow\">here</a>. <em>*This does not apply to students who are on the 2014-2016 or prior catalogs*</em></p>\n<p><strong>When should I attach the certificate?</strong></p>\n<p>As soon as possible. Once you have applied for the certificate and been approved via SAN, please contact your advisor and ask them to officially attach the certificate to your degree profile. It must be attached and set to pursuing in order to gain access to the upper division Elements courses.</p>\n<p><strong>I applied for the certificate and my advisor says it's attached, but it's not set to pursuing. What do I do?</strong></p>\n<p>Email <a href=\"mailto:elements@cs.utexas.edu\" rel=\"nofollow\">elements@cs.utexas.edu</a> with your name and EID and ask that your certificate be set to pursuing.</p>\n<p><strong>The certificate is attached and set to pursuing; do I have priority for the Elements courses?</strong></p>\n<p>No, you have <em>access&nbsp;</em>to register for or waitlist&nbsp;the upper division courses.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><strong>What if I am on the 2014-2016 or prior catalog?</strong></p>\n<p>Students following the 2014-2016 or prior catalogs do not have the function of having the certificate attached to their degree profile, so please email <a href=\"mailto:elements@cs.utexas.edu\" rel=\"nofollow\">elements@cs.utexas.edu</a> prior to registration in order to gain further instructions on how to access the Elements courses.</p>\n<p><strong>Can the certificate be made to \"Stand Alone\"?</strong></p>\n<p>No, we will not make certificate Stand Alone; they need to be attached to a degree profile and set to pursuing.</p>\n<p><strong>Am I required to take CS 303E, or an approved equivalent?</strong></p>\n<p>Yes. All students seeking the Elements certificate are required to take CS 303E, or an approved equivalent. Students who have AP credit for CS 312 may claim that course and it be counted towards the certificate.</p>\n<p><strong>Am I required to take CS 313E, or an approved equivalent?</strong></p>\n<p>Students who are on the 2016-2018 catalog (and beyond) for their major and seeking the 18-hour certificate are required to take CS 313E, or an approved equivalent.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><strong>If I have an approved equivalent for CS 303E and/or CS 313E, how will I be able to register for upper division Elements courses? </strong></p>\n<p>It is very likely that you will need a pre-req waiver in this case. Please email <a href=\"mailto:elements@cs.utexas.edu\" rel=\"nofollow\">elements@cs.utexas.edu</a>: provide your name, EID, which course is substituting for the pre-req, and which course you will like to be waived for. We will respond to you ASAP once we\u2019ve created a pre-req waiver, and it is up to you to register for the course on your own. A pre-req waiver does not guarantee a spot in the course. You will only be granted a pre-req waiver for an <strong>APPROVED</strong> course substitution. Here's an example of a pre-req waiver request:</p>\n<p>Name: John Smith</p>\n<p>eID: JH123</p>\n<p>Substitution: EE 312 for CS 303E</p>\n<p>Course(s) to be waived: CS 324E and CS 327E</p>\n<p><strong>Which upper division courses do not have CS 313E as a pre-req?</strong></p>\n<p>As of the moment, the CS 324E and&nbsp;327E topics courses only require CS 303E as a pre-req. Many Elements courses require CS 313E as a pre-req, which is why it is highly recommended that students take the course prior to taking upper division Elements courses. We will not waive pre-reqs if students do not have an approved pre-req course.</p>\n<p><strong>If I took CS 303E already, does that mean I can no longer take CS 302?</strong></p>\n<p>Correct. CS 302 will not be an option once you have credit for CS 303E, or an approved equivalent. Additionally, CS 302 will not count towards the Elements certificate if the course is taken after you've received credit for CS 303E, or an approved equivalent. Students following the 2016-2018 and newer catalogs cannot use CS 302 towards the certificate.</p>\n<p><strong>When should I start taking courses for the Elements certificate?</strong></p>\n<p>In order to have the best chance at completing the Elements of Computing certificate by graduation, students should take at least one Elements course each semester beginning in the sophomore year.&nbsp; Elements courses have limited seating, and students will not be given special priority when registering. Students must have at least 50% of the certificate completed if they wish to complete any remaining coursework after graduation; additionally, students must complete all requirements and apply for certification within one academic year after graduation.</p>\n<p><strong>May I take any Elements courses pass/fail?</strong></p>\n<p>No. All Elements courses, and any approved equivalents counting towards the Elements certificate, will need to be taken for a letter grade. You will need to receive a C-, or better, in order for the course(s) to count towards the Elements certificate.</p>\n<p><strong>How many Elements courses are offered each semester? Are you expecting to add more courses to the course schedule?</strong></p>\n<p>The number of Elements courses offered each semester varies. It is highly possible that we are not expecting to add more courses to the course schedule; however, please check the course schedule periodically because any changes will reflect on the course schedule.</p>\n<p><strong>What is the max number of Elements upper division courses I can take in a semester?</strong></p>\n<p>Students have the opportunity to take a max of three Elements upper division courses in a semester; this is highly unlikely though due to the demand of the courses.</p>\n<p><strong>Can I register for three Elements upper division&nbsp;courses during the initial registration period?</strong></p>\n<p>No.</p>\n<p><strong>I can only register for two Elements courses during the first registration period, so when can I add a third course to my schedule?</strong></p>\n<p>Students have the potential to&nbsp;add or waitlist a third Elements course&nbsp;during the add/drop period.&nbsp;</p>\n<p><strong>What does it mean if a course is \u201cApproved through\u201d a specified semester?</strong></p>\n<p>That means if you took the course on or prior to the listed semester, it is an approved substitution for the Elements certificate regardless of when you apply for certification.&nbsp; Taking the course past the semester will need approval from the Elements faculty advisor in order for it to be a valid substitution.</p>\n<p><strong>What if I would like to petition a non-approved course substitution for the Elements certificate?</strong></p>\n<p>You must be registered for the course, and have received or will be expecting to receive a C-, or better, letter grade. Please print, read through, follow all the instructions, and fill out the <a href=\"/sites/default/files/images/Elements%20Course%20Substitution%20Form_4.pdf\" rel=\"nofollow\">Elements Course Substitution Form</a>. These petitions are not guaranteed for approval, and you will be sent a <a href=\"https://utdirect.utexas.edu/san/index.WBX\" rel=\"nofollow\">SAN</a> once the Elements faculty advisor makes a final decision. The final decision may not be appealed.</p>\n<p><strong>How do I go about getting a transfer course evaluated as a course substitution?</strong></p>\n<p>If the transfer course in question does not transfer directly as an approved substitution, then you will need to submit an <a href=\"/sites/default/files/images/Elements%20Course%20Substitution%20Form_4.pdf\" rel=\"nofollow\">Elements Course Substitution Form</a> after you\u2019ve transferred the course in to UT. This means you must have completed the course with a C-, or better, and send in your transcript to be evaluated by UT\u2019s Office of Admissions. Once that evaluation is on your records, please submit the <a href=\"/sites/default/files/images/Elements%20Course%20Substitution%20Form_4.pdf\" rel=\"nofollow\">Elements Course Substitution Form</a>. Please print, read through, follow all the instructions, and fill out the form. These petitions are not guaranteed for approval, and you will be sent a <a href=\"https://utdirect.utexas.edu/san/index.WBX\" rel=\"nofollow\">SAN</a> once the Elements faculty advisor makes a final decision. The final decision may not be appealed.</p>\n<p><strong>What courses count as substitutions?</strong></p>\n<p>Courses that could be considered as substitutions are anything taken outside the Department of Computer Science here at the University of Texas at Austin. This includes study abroad courses even if they are approved as Elements equivalents (i.e. CS 329E).</p>\n<p><strong>What are the requirements for the 18-hour transcripted certificate?</strong></p>\n<p>The new requirements are for students who are in the 2016-2018 catalog (and beyond) for their major. If you are not sure what catalog year you are under, please contact your academic advisor. The new requirements for the 18-hour certificate include the following:</p>\n<ul><li>CS 303E, or an approved equivalent, is required.</li>\n<li>CS 313E, or an approved equivalent, is required.</li>\n<li>At least twelve hours of upper division Elements elective courses, or approved equivalents, are&nbsp;required.</li>\n<li>Only two course substitutions are permitted.</li>\n<li>All courses must be taken for a letter grade with a grade of C-, or better.\n<ul><li><em>*Note: You may claim AP credit for CS 312*</em></li>\n</ul></li>\n</ul><p><strong>When do I submit the survey&nbsp;for the Elements certification?</strong></p>\n<p>Students may <a href=\"https://utexas.qualtrics.com/jfe/form/SV_0HF54R4PKdh1Hz7\" rel=\"nofollow\">apply for certification</a> on the semester they are completing their last requirements. If you missed the deadline, please submit the application prior to the next deadline so that we may certify you for that semester:</p>\n<ul><li><strong>Spring:</strong> May 1 by 5 PM</li>\n<li><strong>Summer:</strong> August 1 by 5 PM</li>\n<li><strong>Fall: </strong>Dec 1 by 5 PM</li>\n</ul><p>Once you've completed all the certificate requirements, applied for certification, and received approval on your certification form, you will receive a digital Elements of Computing certificate and completion letter at the end of the certification process.
