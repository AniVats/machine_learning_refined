class=\"element-invisible\">You are here</h2><div class=\"breadcrumb\"><a href=\"/\">Home</a> \u00bb <a href=\"/directory\">Directory</a> \u00bb <a href=\"/directory/faculty\">Faculty</a></div><h1 field-label-hidden\"><div class=\"field-items\"><div class=\"field-item even\"><img src=\"https://www.umsec.umn.edu/sites/www.umsec.umn.edu/files/styles/large/public/profile_11.jpg?itok=yk1Wn_MO\" class=\"field-label\">Education:&nbsp;</div><div class=\"field-items\"><div class=\"field-item even\"><p>M.S. Computer Science and Engineering from the Royal Institute of Technology, Sweden, 1988.</p>\n<p>Ph.D. Computer Science, University of California at Irvine, 1994.</p>\n</div></div></div><div class=\"field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-above\"><div class=\"field-label\">Biography:&nbsp;</div><div class=\"field-items\"><div class=\"field-item even\"><p>Professor Mats Heimdahl specializes in software engineering and safety critical systems. He is the director of the University of Minnesota Software Engineering Center (UMSEC).</p>\n<p>Heimdahl is the recipient of the National Science Foundation's CAREER award, a McKnight Land-Grant Professorship and the McKnight Presidential Fellow award at the University of Minnesota, and the University of Minnesota Award for Outstanding Contributions to Post-Baccalaureate, Graduate, and Professional Education.</p>\n</div></div></div><div class=\"field field-name-field-research field-type-text-long field-label-above\"><div class=\"field-label\">Research:&nbsp;</div><div class=\"field-items\"><div class=\"field-item even\"><p>Software is increasingly involved in our lives; software controls physical systems ranging from microwave ovens and watches to nuclear power plants, aircraft, and cars. Computer-related failures can, in many of these applications, have catastrophic effects.</p>\n<p>My research group, the Critical Systems Research Group (CriSys), is conducting research in software engineering and is investigating methods and tools to help us develop software with predictable behavior free from defects.</p>\n<p>Research in this area spans all aspects of system development ranging from concept formation and requirements specification, through design and implementation, to testing and maintenance. In particular, we are currently investigating model-based software development for critical systems.</p>\n<p>Specifically, we are focusing on how to use various static verification techniques to assure that software requirements models possess desirable properties, how to correctly generate production code from software requirements models, how to validate models, and how to effectively use the models in the testing process.</p>\n</div></div></div><div class=\"field field-name-field-interests field-type-text-long field-label-above\"><div class=\"field-label\">Interests:&nbsp;</div><div class=\"field-items\"><div class=\"field-item even\"><p>Software engineering and safety critical systems.</p>\n</div></div></div>  </div>\n\n  \n  \n</div>\n  </div>\n</div>\n<div id=\"block-views-recent-publications-block\" class=\"block block-views\">\n\n    <h2>Recent Publications</h2>\n  \n  <div class=\"content\">\n    <div class=\"view view-recent-publications view-id-recent_publications view-display-id-block view-dom-id-8027afd2b93d227d627191b070f74249\">\n        \n  \n  \n      <div class=\"view-content\">\n        <div class=\"views-row views-row-1 views-row-odd views-row-first\">\n    <div id=\"node-1123\" class=\"node node-publication node-teaser clearfix\">\n\n  \n        <h2><a href=\"/publications/Contract-discovery-black-box-components\">Contract discovery from black-box components</a></h2>\n    \n  \n  <div class=\"content\">\n    <div class=\"field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden\"><div class=\"field-items\"><div class=\"field-item even\">Complex computer-controlled systems are commonly constructed in a middle-out fashion where existing subsystems and available components have a significant influence on system architecture and drive design decisions. During system design, the architect must verify that the components, put together as specified in the architecture, will achieve the desired system behavior. This typically leads to further design modifications or adjustments to requirements triggering another iteration of the design-verify cycle.</div></div></div>  </div>\n\n  <ul class=\"links inline\"><li class=\"node-readmore first last\"><a href=\"/publications/Contract-discovery-black-box-components\" rel=\"tag\" title=\"Contract discovery from black-box components\">Read more<span class=\"element-invisible\"> about Contract discovery from black-box components</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n  \n</div>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"views-row views-row-2 views-row-even\">\n    <div id=\"node-1005\" class=\"node node-publication node-teaser clearfix\">\n\n  \n        <h2><a href=\"/publications/Toward-Rigorous-Object-Code-Coverage-Criteria\">Toward Rigorous Object-Code Coverage Criteria</a></h2>\n    \n  \n  <div class=\"content\">\n    <div class=\"field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden\"><div class=\"field-items\"><div class=\"field-item even\">Object-branch coverage (OBC) is often used as a measure of the thoroughness of tests suites, augmenting or substituting source-code based structural criteria such as branch coverage and modified condition/decision coverage (MC/DC). In addition, with the increasing use of third-party components for which source-code access may be unavailable, robust object-code coverage criteria are essential to assess how well the components are exercised during testing.</div></div></div>  </div>\n\n  <ul class=\"links inline\"><li class=\"node-readmore first last\"><a href=\"/publications/Toward-Rigorous-Object-Code-Coverage-Criteria\" rel=\"tag\" title=\"Toward Rigorous Object-Code Coverage Criteria\">Read more<span class=\"element-invisible\"> about Toward Rigorous Object-Code Coverage Criteria</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n  \n</div>\n  </div>\n  <div class=\"views-row views-row-3 views-row-odd views-row-last\">\n    <div id=\"node-1016\" class=\"node node-publication node-teaser clearfix\">\n\n  \n        <h2><a href=\"/publications/Architecture-Modeling-and-Analysis-Safety\">Architecture Modeling and Analysis for Safety Engineering</a></h2>\n    \n  \n  <div class=\"content\">\n    <div class=\"field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden\"><div class=\"field-items\"><div class=\"field-item even\">Architecture description languages such as AADL allow systems engineers to specify the structure of system architectures and perform several analyses over them, including schedulability, resource analysis, and information flow. In addition, they permit system-level requirements to be specified and analyzed early in the development process of airborne and ground-based systems. These tools can also be used to perform safety analysis based on the system architecture and initial functional decomposition.</div></div></div>  </div>\n\n  <ul class=\"links inline\"><li
