He has a particular interest in\napplications in medical imaging (image compression for\ntelemedicine, tomographic and MRI reconstruction, etc.).</p>\n<p>The selected publications below indicate some of these\ninterests. The first paper is a study of radiologist\nperformance in interpreting wavelet-compressed mammographic\nimages. The compression method was designed specifically to\nkeep image features that are needed to interpret\nmammograms. The results showed that after compressing the\nimages over 50-to-1 (on average; compression rates ranged\nfrom 14-to-1 for mammograms with dense structures to over\n2000-to-1 for mammograms with few optical structures)\nradiologists interpreted the compressed mammograms <em>more\naccurately</em> than they interpreted the originals. Such\neffective data compression may allow mammographic\nscreenings to be performed by technicians in areas\ntraditionally underserved by the medical community, with\nthe data sent over regular phone lines to radiologists in\nmedical centers for interpretation.</p>\n<p>The second paper introduces a new numerical method for\nimage smoothing based on the total variation norm, as\nintroduced in a famous 1992 paper by Rudin, Osher, and\nFatemi. Our new method is more isotropic and exhibits less\nsmoothing than previous methods. Finally, the third paper\nwith my student Jingyue Wang gives the first provable error\nbounds for Rudin-Osher-Fatemi image smoothing, including\nthe new method introduced in the second paper.</p></div>\n<div><h3>Selected Publications</h3><div style=\"margin-bottom: 1em;\">Maria Kallergi, Bradley J.
