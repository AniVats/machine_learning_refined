Previously, I received my PhD from <a href=\"https://cs.berkeley.edu/\">UC\nBerkeley</a> where I was advised by \n<a href=\"https://www2.eecs.berkeley.edu/Faculty/Homepages/shenker.html\">Scott Shenker</a> and worked in the <a href=\"http://netsys.cs.berkeley.edu/\">NetSys\nLab</a>. Even earlier,  I received a Sc.B. with honors in Math-CS from <a href=\"https://cs.brown.edu\">Brown</a>.</p>\n\n<p>My research interests lie in finding lightweight mechanisms for achieving <em>correctness</em> in systems (broadly defined). My\n<a href=\"assets/dissertation/dissertation-updated.pdf\">dissertation</a> looked at how to <em>implement</em> and \n<em>execute</em> network functions; and how to <em>verify</em> correctness for \nNFV (network function virtualization) deployments. A <a href=\"projects/\">selection of past projects</a> and <a href=\"#publications\">publications</a> \ncan be found on this site.</p>\n\n<p>Before starting at NYU, I spent a year as a software developer at <a href=\"https://nefeli.io\">Nefeli Networks</a>, a startup\nfocused on solutions for Network Function orchestration. Between Brown and Berkeley I spent a few years working on the <a href=\"https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/project/singularity/\">Midori</a> kernel at\nMicrosoft.</p>\n\n<!--<table><tr><td style=\"background-color:#e7dacb;color:#4a89aa;font-size:1.2em\">-->\n<!--I am looking for PhD students interested in working with me.
