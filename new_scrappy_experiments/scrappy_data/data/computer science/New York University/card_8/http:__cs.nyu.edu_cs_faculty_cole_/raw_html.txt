My education began in\n      France, and was continued in England for the latter part of\n      primary (elementary) school and for secondary school (at Ealing\n      Grammar School for Boys, now closed). For my undergraduate\n      studies, I went to University College, Oxford,&nbsp; receiving my\n      BA in Mathematics in 1978. I then came to America to study for a\n      Ph.D. in Computer Science at Cornell University; this was\n      supervised by John Hopcroft and completed in 1982. After this I\n      came to NYU as an assistant professor. I was promoted to full\n      professor in 1990. I served as department chair from 1994-2000.\n      and as interim director of the Courant Institute from 2015-2016. I\n      was named a Guggenheim Fellow for the 1988-89 academic year, an\n      ACM Fellow in 1998, and a Silver Professor of Computer Science in\n      2011.
