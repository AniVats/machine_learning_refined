A broad range of topics is covered, including\ndistribution of prime integers, Erdos Magic, random graphs, Ramsey numbers, \nand asymptotic geometry.\nThe author is a disciple of Paul Erdos, who taught him about Asymptopia. Primes less than n, graphs with v vertices, random walks of t steps--Erdos was\nfascinated by the limiting behavior as the variables approached, but never reached, infinity. Asymptotics is very much an art. The various functions nlnn,\nn^2, (ln n)/n, \\sqrt{\\ln n},  ln(ln n) all have distinct personalities. \nErdos knew these functions as personal friends. It is the author's hope that these insights may\nbe passed on, that the reader may similarly feel which function has the right temperament for a given task. \n</p><p>\nAsymptopia is a beautiful world.
