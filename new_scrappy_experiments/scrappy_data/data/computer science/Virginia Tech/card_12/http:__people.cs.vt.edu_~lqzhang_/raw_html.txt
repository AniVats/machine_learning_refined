Making sense of the \"Big Data\" poses grave challenge yet exciting research\nopportunities.\nOur research is about developing computational and statistical tools that can process and analyze various kinds of complex bio-data efficiently and mine the data (e.g., human genomic data, cancer data, and metagenomics data) to \nhelp address important biological and biomedical questions,\nthus facilitating the process from data to knowledge discovery.\n<br><br>\nOur interest falls into the broad category of computational omics, for various omics data,\nsuch as\ncancer genomics, metagenomics, evolutionary and comparative genomics, and \npopulation genomics.\nSome current projects include:\n(1). develop computational pipelines and databases\n for analyzing and mining metagenomics data, to understand the impact of microbes on humans and our environment.\n(2). develop short read mapping tool for whole genome bisulfite sequencing data and statistical tool for variant calling and methylation calling in bisulfite short reads.\n(3). predict the effect of genetic variations such as SNPs and indels using hidden Markov models.\n(4). develop tools for studying indels. In particular, we have been developing an indel annotation \nsystem, the adoption of which can eliminate the redundancy problem,\na serious one in indel data storage.\n\n<!--\n\n<li>\nCancer bioinformatics: Develop analysis methods and programs for various kinds of data in cancer.\n</li>\n<Li>Evolutionary and Comparative Genomics: Compare genomes to infer evolutionary events and forces \nshaping the genomes, with a special interest on the evolution of duplicated genes in both primary sequences\nand function.</Li>\n<Li>Population Genomics: Develop poplation genetic models that incorporate the non-indepedence of mutations with different fitness effect to understand the evolution of genomes.</Li>\n<Li>Computational Biology: Develop open source software and online databases for analyzing high throughput \ndata.
