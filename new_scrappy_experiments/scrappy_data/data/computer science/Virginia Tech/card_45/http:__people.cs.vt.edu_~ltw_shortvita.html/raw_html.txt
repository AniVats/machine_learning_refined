The overall goal is to provide\nsophisticated mathematical software, justified by rigorous mathematical\nanalysis, to attack significant practical engineering and scientific\nproblems.  An example is the <a href=\"http://www.cs.vt.edu/~ltw/hompack/\">\nHOMPACK</a> project that was designed to make\nmathematically obscure homotopy methods available to the typical\nengineer, and to provide production quality software to the user\ncommunity.  HOMPACK is now in use at hundreds of sites from Australia to\nYugoslavia, and it has been successfully applied to problems in fluid\nmechanics, solid mechanics, composite materials, chemical engineering,\neconomics, biology, robotics, computer vision, optimal control, circuit\ndesign, machine learning, and nonlinear optimization.\n</p><p>Over the years, I have done\nmathematical analyses in the areas of approximation theory, homotopy\ntheory, optimization, nonlinear equations, and computer vision.\nRecently, my focus has been on the application of <a href=\"http://www.cs.vt.edu/~lasca\">parallel computation</a> to\nlarge scale, <a href=\"http://www.dept.aoe.vt.edu/research/groups/afrl/\">interdisciplinary\nengineering</a> problems, and to <a href=\"http://bioinformatics.cs.vt.edu\">bioinformatics</a>.</p><p>\n\n</p><p></p><hr>\n<a href=\"http://www.wseas.org\"><font size=\"4\">\n<img border=\"0\" src=\"http://www.worldses.org/logotype.gif\" width=\"30\" height=\"34\" align=\"left\">www.wseas.org</font></a>\n\n\n</body></html>"
