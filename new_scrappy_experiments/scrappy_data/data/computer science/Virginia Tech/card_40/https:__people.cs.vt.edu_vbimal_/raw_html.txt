              <div class=\"col-md-9\">\n                    <div class=\"row\" id=\"latestupdates\">\n                        <div class=\"col-md-12\">\n                            <p>My research interests broadly cover security and privacy aspects of large online services. I am particularly interested in the emerging space at the intersection of <strong>machine learning and security</strong>. Three research directions include: (1) Attacks using ML: Investigating how recent developments in ML can be misused to abuse online services. (2) Attacks on ML systems: Understanding vulnerabilities of ML systems (e.g., Face recognition) and improving robustness. (3) ML for better security/privacy: Leveraging advances in ML to build better defenses against attacks on online services.\n                            <br><br>\n\n                            I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the&nbsp;<a href=\"https://www.mpi-sws.org/\">Max Planck Institute for Software Systems</a> where I was advised by&nbsp;<a href=\"https://www.mpi-sws.org/~gummadi/\">Prof. Krishna P. Gummadi</a>                                and&nbsp;<a href=\"https://www.ccs.neu.edu/home/amislove/\">Prof. Alan Mislove</a>. Before joining VT, I spent ~2 years as a Postdoc in <a href=\"http://sandlab.cs.uchicago.edu/\">SAND lab</a> (at <a href=\"https://www.cs.ucsb.edu/\">UCSB</a> and <a href=\"https://www.cs.uchicago.edu/\">UChicago</a>) working with <a href=\"http://people.cs.uchicago.edu/~ravenben/\">Prof. Ben Y. Zhao</a>. Prior to that, I spent a year as a Researcher at&nbsp;<a href=\"https://www.bell-labs.com/\">Nokia Bell Labs, Stuttgart</a>. \n\n                                \n</p>\n                        <p class=\"text-danger\">\n                            <strong>We have open positions for students in Fall 2020!
