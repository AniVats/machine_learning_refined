Cameron, Virginia Tech</h1>\n\t</header><!-- .entry-header -->\n\n\t<div class=\"entry-content\">\n\t\t<p><strong><a href=\"http://people.cs.vt.edu/~cameron/\">Kirk W. Cameron</a></strong>, Ph.D. is a Professor of Computer Science and a Research Fellow in the College of Engineering at Virginia Tech. He was recently appointed the first Computer Science Associate Department Head for Research and Engagement where, with the help of five talented staff members, he oversees department alumni and corporate relations, communications, and marketing. He was Associate Department Head and Graduate Program Director from 2014-2017. From 2017-2018, Prof. Cameron held a Distinguished Visiting Fellowship at Queen\u2019s University Belfast from the U.K. Royal Academy of Engineering. He is Director of the <a href=\"http://stack.cs.vt.edu\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">stack@cs Center for Computer Systems</a> and the Scalable Performance Laboratory; a Distinguished Member of the ACM; and an associate editor for the <a href=\"https://www.journals.elsevier.com/journal-of-parallel-and-distributed-computing/editorial-board\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Journal of Parallel and Distributed Computing</a>&nbsp;and for <a href=\"https://www.computer.org/web/tpds\">IEEE Transactions on Parallel and Distributed Computing</a>.</p>\n<p>The central theme of his research is to improve power and performance efficiency in high performance computing (HPC) systems and applications. Accolades for his work include NSF and DOE Career Awards, IBM and AMD Faculty Awards, and being named Innovator of the Week by Bloomberg Businessweek Magazine.&nbsp;He pioneered Green Computing (<a href=\"https://www.top500.org/green500/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Green500</a>, <a href=\"https://www.spec.org/power/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">SPECPower</a>, PowerPack, grano.la) and his power measurement and management software has been&nbsp;downloaded by more than 500,000 people in 160+ countries. &nbsp;He is a recipient of the&nbsp;<a href=\"http://www.hpdc.org/2017/awards/best-paper-award/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">HPDC 2017 Best Paper Award</a> and his educational <a href=\"http://seemore.cs.vt.edu\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">SeeMore</a> kinetic cluster (pictured above) was&nbsp;named the <a href=\"https://www.raspberrypi.org/magpi-issues/MagPi50.pdf\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">second best RaspberryPi project of all time by MagPi Magazine</a>.&nbsp;His work consistently appears in&nbsp;The New York Times, The Guardian, Time, Newsweek, etc.</p>\n<h2><strong>PUBLICATIONS AND RESEARCH PHILOSOPHY</strong></h2>\n<p>For me, <strong>research is about breaking new ground</strong> and sharing fundamental theories, conjectures, and findings that one day I believe will change the way we compute and ultimately improve the human condition. I also like to follow my instincts and I strive to do things that are research-creative and seem <a href=\"https://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=0347683\">disruptive</a> or&nbsp;<a href=\"http://seemore.cs.vt.edu\">crazy</a> at first. As time has marched on, I\u2019ve developed&nbsp;<strong>three tenets to my research</strong>: 1) Do something cool, exciting, fun, and (just maybe) transformative; 2) Find someone that\u2019s crazy enough to join in; and 3) Don\u2019t work with jerks. That\u2019s not to say all the work I\u2019ve done has followed these principles; in contrast, these tenets evolved from my mistakes over the years more than anything else.</p>\n<p>If you want to see everything I\u2019ve done, look at my:</p>\n<p><a href=\"https://dblp.uni-trier.de/pers/hd/c/Cameron:Kirk_W=\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">DBLP entry</a></p>\n<p><a href=\"https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=ZH1pa0EAAAAJ&amp;hl=en\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Google Scholar Page</a></p>\n<p><a href=\"http://wordpress.cs.vt.edu/cameron/wp-content/uploads/sites/79/2019/02/cv020319.pdf\">CV</a></p>\n<p>If you want to understand the impact my research has had on computing, keep reading or jump to: <a href=\"#green-computing\">Green Computing</a>, <a href=\"#power-performance-modeling\">Power-Performance Modeling</a>, <a href=\"#computer-science-education\">Computer Science Education</a>, or <a href=\"#io-performance\">I/O Performance</a></p>\n<h2><strong>IMPACT ON GREEN COMPUTING</strong></h2>\n<p><a name=\"green-computing\"></a></p>\n<h3><strong>[Pioneering Green HPC and Datacenter Research]</strong></h3>\n<p>National Science Foundation, \u201c<a href=\"https://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward?AWD_ID=0614705\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">CAREER: High-performance, distributed power-aware computing</a>,\u201d $402,203, 5 years. 2/1/04-1/31/09. Kirk W. Cameron (PI). Personal share: $402,203. <strong>If there is anything I\u2019m widely known for, it is green HPC and data centers. Originally entitled \u201cHigh-performance, power-aware computing\u201d or HPPAC, this proposal, written in 2003 and based on ideas I first had in 2000-2001, is the first known submitted and funded research project in Green HPC \u2014 energy efficient large-scale systems. The research plan was years ahead of the community and the resulting work inspired hundreds (perhaps thousands) of researchers worldwide to study energy use in data centers and supercomputers. This work led to my co-founding <a href=\"https://www.top500.org/green500/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">Green500.org</a>, my being a founding member of <a href=\"https://www.spec.org/power/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">SPECPower</a>, my early influence on the <a href=\"https://www.energystar.gov/products/office_equipment/enterprise_servers\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"noopener\">energy star program for servers</a>, and the co-founding of&nbsp;energy-efficient software startup MiserWare, Inc. The original proposal name was adopted by colleagues for the IEEE HPPAC workshop at IPDPS that will celebrate 15 years in 2019.</strong></p>\n<h3><strong>[First Green HPC Publications]</strong></h3>\n<p>Cameron, K.W., Ge, R., Feng, X., Varner, D., Jones, C.
