Sohi</h1>\n       John P. Morgridge Professor<br>\n       E. David Cronon Professor of Computer Sciences<br>\n<a href=\"http://www.cs.wisc.edu/arch/uwarch/\">UW-Madison Computer Architecture Group</a><br>\nDepartments of <a href=\"http://www.cs.wisc.edu/\">Computer Sciences</a><br>\nand <a href=\"http://www.engr.wisc.edu/ece/index.html\">Electrical\nand Computer Engineering<br>\n</a><a href=\"http://www.wisc.edu/\">University of Wisconsin-Madison</a>\n\n</td>\n\n\n\n  \n  <td align=\"RIGHT\" valign=\"CENTER\" nowrap=\"\">\n    <img src=\"http://www.cs.wisc.edu/~sohi/Sohi_Pic1.jpg\" alt=\"2006 Photo of Gurindar S. Sohi by Bob Rashid\" border=\"0\" align=\"CENTER\"><br>\n       <font size=\"-2\">By Bob Rashid in 2006</font></td>\n</tr>\n</tbody></table>\n\n\n\n<!-- BEGIN the body of the web page here -->\n\n<font size=\"2\">\n\n\n<b>Research Interests</b><br>\nComputer architecture, parallel computing, memory systems,\nand performance evaluation.\n</font></p><p><font size=\"2\">\n<b>Teaching Interests:</b>\nComputer organization (CS/ECE 354 and 552), computer architecture (752),\nand parallel computer architecture (757).\n</font></p><p><font size=\"2\">\n<b>Ph.D.: Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Illinois, 1985.</b>\n\n</font></p><p><font size=\"2\">\n<b>Biography:</b>\nGuri Sohi received a Ph.D in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Illinois,\nand has been a faculty member at the University of Wisconsin-Madison since 1985.\nHe is currently the John P. Morgridge Professor and the E. David Cronon Professor of Computer Sciences\nin the Computer Sciences and Electrical and Computer Engineering departments.\nHe served as the Department Chair of the Computer Sciences department from 2004 through 2008.\n\n</font></p><p><font size=\"2\">\nSohi's research has been in the design of high-performance computer systems. He has co-authored\nseveral papers and patents that have influenced both researchers and commercial microprocessors.\nIn the mid 1980s, while most computer architects were investigating in-order processors,\nhe investigated out-of-order processors. His paper \"Instruction Issue Logic for High-Performance,\nInterruptible Pipelined Processors\" (in ISCA 1987) articulated a model for a dynamically-scheduled\nprocessor supporting precise exceptions, a model that was widely adopted by several microprocessor\nmanufacturers. (This paper, and the journal version in IEEE Trans. on Computers, March 1990, have\nbeen referenced by over 150 U.S. patents.\n\n<a href=\"http://patft1.uspto.gov/netacgi/nph-Parser?Sect1=PTO2&amp;Sect2=HITOFF&amp;p=1&amp;u=%2Fnetahtml%2FPTO%2Fsearch-bool.html&amp;r=0&amp;f=S&amp;l=50&amp;TERM1=Sohi&amp;FIELD1=OREF&amp;co1=AND&amp;TERM2=Instruction+Issue+Logic&amp;FIELD2=OREF&amp;d=PTXT\">Click here to see the list.</a>)\nHis paper \"High Bandwidth Data Memory Systems for\nSuperscalar Processors\" (in ASPLOS 1991) argued for non-blocking (or lockup-free) caches,\nand was instrumental in influencing high-end microprocessors to switch from blocking to\nnon-blocking caches. In the early 1990s while other computer architects started investigating\nout-of-order processors he proposed the concept of multiscalar processors and thread-level speculation\nin his papers \"The Expandable Split Window Paradigm for Exploiting Fine-Grain Parallelism\" (in ISCA 1992)\nand \"Multiscalar Processors\" (in ISCA 1995). Thread-level speculation and its variants are currently one of\nthe most active areas of research in computer architecture. His paper \"Dynamic Speculation and Synchronization\nof Data Dependences\" (in ISCA 1997) introduced the idea of memory dependence prediction, an idea that was\nused in the Alpha processor designs and is being considered by others.  His paper \"Dynamic Instruction Reuse\"\n(in ISCA 1997) proposed the concept of instruction reuse, another area of active research.  He and his\nstudents also had the first academic proposal for trace caches.  Sohi's research group also developed the\nSimplescalar simulator, a simulation toolset that is widely used for research and instruction.\n\n</font></p><p><font size=\"2\">\nSohi has interacted heavily with industry.  Over the years he has discussed his research with architects\nand given talks in design groups at most of the leading microprocessor manufacturers, including\nDigital Equipment, HaL, Hewlett-Packard, IBM, Intel, MIPS, Motorola, Silicon Graphics, and Sun Microsystems.\n\n</font></p><p><font size=\"2\">\nHe edited \"25 Years of the International Symposium on Computer Architecture - Selected Papers\" published\nby ACM, and recently co-edited (with Mark Hill and Norm Jouppi) \"Readings in Computer Architecture\"\npublished by Morgan Kaufmann Publishers.\n\n</font></p><p><font size=\"2\">\nSohi has graduated 18 Ph.D students, many of whom currently hold academic positions at leading research\nuniversities (Illinois, Maryland, Michigan, Pennsylvania, Purdue, and Toronto). They include six winners\nof NSF CAREER awards and a winner of a Sloan Research Fellowship. He continues to lead a research\ngroup investigating different models for speculative multithreading, approximate programs,\nvalue communication prediction, chip multiprocessing, and other innovations for future microprocessors.\n\n</font></p><p><font size=\"2\">\nHe received the 1999 ACM SIGARCH Maurice Wilkes award \"for seminal contributions in the areas of\nhigh issue rate processors and instruction level parallelism\".  At the University of Wisconsin he was\nselected as a Vilas Associate in 1997, won the WARF Kellett Mid-Career Faculty Researcher award in 2000\nand was selected a WARF Named Professor (E. David Cronon Professor of Computer Sciences) in 2007.\nHe is a Fellow of the ACM and a Fellow of the IEEE and a member of the National Academy of Engineering.\n\n\n\n<!-- END of body of the web page
