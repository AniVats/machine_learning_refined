Hill (http://www.cs.wisc.edu/~markhill) is \nJohn P. Morgridge Professor and\nGene M. Amdahl Professor of Computer Sciences\nat the University of Wisconsin-Madison,\nwhere he also has a courtesy appointment in Electrical and Computer Engineering.\nHis research interests include parallel-computer system design,\nmemory system design, and computer simulation. \nHill's work is highly collaborative with over 160 co-authors\nand especially his long-time colleague David A. Wood.\nHe received the 2019 Eckert-Mauchly Award and is a fellow of IEEE and the ACM. \nHe serves as Chair of the Computer Community Consortium (2018-20) and\nserved as Wisconsin Computer Sciences Department Chair 2014-2017.\nHill has a PhD in computer science from the University of California, Berkeley. \n\n</p><p>\n<b>Longer Biography:</b>\nMark D. Hill (http://www.cs.wisc.edu/~markhill) is \nJohn P. Morgridge Professor,\nand Gene M. Amdahl Professor of Computer Sciences\nat the University of Wisconsin-Madison.\nHe has long had a UW-Madison Electrical and Computer Engineering\ncourtesy appointment.\nHe serves as Chair of the\n<a href=\"http://cra.org/ccc/\">Computer Community Consortium</a> (CCC)(2018-20) and served as Vice Chair (2016-18).\nHe is a member of the <a href=\"https://cra.org\">Computing Research Association</a> Board of Directors (2018-20). \nHe served as Wisconsin Computer Sciences Department Chair July 2014 to June 2017.\n\n</p><p>\nDr. Hill's research targets computer design and evaluation.\nHe has made contributions to parallel computer system design\n(e.g., memory consistency models and cache coherence),\nmemory system design (caches and translation buffers),\ncomputer simulation (parallel systems and memory systems),\nsoftware (e.g., page tables and cache-conscious optimizations),\ndeterministic replay and transactional memory. \nFor example, he is the inventor of the widely-used <i>3C model</i>\nof cache behavior (<i>compulsory,</i> <i>capacity,</i>\nand <i>conflict</i> misses), \nand co-inventor of the memory consistency model\n<i>sequential consistency for data-race-free programs</i>\nthat serves as a foundation for the C++ and Java multi-threaded\nmemory specifications. \nHill's work is highly collaborative with over 160 co-authors\nand especially his long-time colleague David A. Wood.\n</p><p>\nHill received the \n<a href=\"https://awards.acm.org/award_winners/hill_2155109\">ACM - IEEE CS Eckert-Mauchly Award in 2019</a>\nfor <i> seminal contributions to the fields of cache memories, memory consistency models, transactional memory, and simulation</i>.\nHe was selected as a John P. Morgridge Endowed Chair of\nUW-Madison Computer Sciences in 2015.\nHe was named an ACM Fellow (2004)\n<i>for contributions to memory consistency models and memory system design</i>,\nelevated to \na Fellow of the IEEE (2000) \n<i>for contributions to cache memory design and analysis,</i>\nand was awarded the ACM SIGARCH Alan Berenbaum Distinguished Service Award in 2009.\nHe was won four important University of Wisconsin-Madison awards: WARF Named Professorship in 2013 (2nd winner from CS), \nKellett in 2010 (3rd winner from CS), Vilas Associate in 2006, and Romnes Fellow in 1997. \nHe co-wrote\n<a href=\"http://www.morganclaypool.com/doi/abs/10.2200/S00346ED1V01Y201104CAC016\">A\nPrimer on Memory Consistency and Cache Coherence</a> in 2011 (downloaded 7000 times),\nco-edited <i>Readings in Computer Architecture</i> in 2000, is co-inventor of\n40 United States patents (several of which have been\nco-issued in the European Union\n&amp; Japan), was an ACM SIGARCH Director (1993-2007),\nwon an NSF Presidential Young Investigator award in 1989,\nand member of informal \n<a href=\"http://pages.cs.wisc.edu/~arch/www/iscabibhall.html\">ISCA</a> and \n<a href=\"http://newsletter.sigmicro.org/micro-hof.txt/view\">MICRO</a> Halls of Fame.\n<!-- BEGIN COMMENT {\n(from\n<A
