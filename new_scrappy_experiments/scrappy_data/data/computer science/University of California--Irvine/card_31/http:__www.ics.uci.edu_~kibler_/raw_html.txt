goal of this course is to introduce students to\nthe bioinformatics research paradigm by studying three significant problems in\nunderstanding the genome. The paradigm consists of starting with a biological\nproblem, biological data and biological knowledge and then building an\napproximate computational model. The model is then applied to real data and its\npredictions are then validated by additional biological experiments. Ideally\nthis cycle repeats. The course will begin with the problem of identifying genes\nfrom sequence data using the GENSCAN program. After genes are identified, their\nfunction will be hypothesized using the BLAST program. How genes are regulated\nwill be answered using programs for finding surprising subsequences. In all of\nthese studies students will work with real data using programs freely available\non the web. The ideas underlying the algorithms, their limitations, and their\nconnection to biology will be stressed. </p>\n\n<p style=\"margin-left:.5in\"><b>Student Projects with draft abstracts</b> </p>\n\n<ul type=\"disc\">\n <ul type=\"circle\">\n  <li class=\"MsoNormal\" style=\"mso-margin-top-alt:auto;mso-margin-bottom-alt:\n      auto;mso-list:l1 level2 lfo2;tab-stops:list 1.0in\">Homosexuality:\n      Determined by genetics or social environment by <i>Mansi Shah and Wendy\n      Kim </i></li>\n  <li class=\"MsoNormal\" style=\"mso-margin-top-alt:auto;mso-margin-bottom-alt:\n      auto;mso-list:l1 level2 lfo2;tab-stops:list 1.0in\">Applying Microarray\n      Technology to predicting Adverse drug reaction in children with acute\n      lymphoblastic leukemia by <i>Ruwani Ekanayake, Amy Henry, and Brittany\n      Horth</i><br>\n      <b>Abstract:</b> Pharmacogenomics, the study of how an individual's\n      genetic inheritance affects the body's response to drugs, has expanded exponentially\n      since the completion of the Human Genome Project in 2003.
