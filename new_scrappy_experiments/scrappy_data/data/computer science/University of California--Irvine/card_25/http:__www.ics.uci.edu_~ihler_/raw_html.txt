\n<div style=\"height: 10px;\"></div>\n<div class=\"box-txt\">\n<p>Graphical models are used to organize and structure probability distributions over large systems, and enable\nefficient approximate or exact reasoning. \nMy group balances developing theoretical and algorithmic advances with applications\nto the real-world systems of our collaborators.\n</p><p><i>Algorithms.</i> One of our main focuses is on finding maxima or computing probabilities using variational\nmethods, including the family of belief propagation (BP) message-passing algorithms.  \nOur contributions include analyzing the convergence and accuracy properties\nof BP, developing new BP-like bounds, extending BP techniques to continuous valued systems, improving\nthe efficiency of \"adaptive\" or incremental inference, and extending variational algorithms to ``mixed''\ninference tasks such as marginal MAP and decision making problems, including influence diagrams (or decision\nnetworks) and distributed team decision problems.\n</p><p><i>Applications.</i> We have applied our algorithms to a wide variety of problems, including tracking\nand understanding data from sensor networks,\nefficient representations for large text corpora, computer vision and image processing, and\ngene expression data in biology.</p>\n<!--\n<ul style=\"margin:0 0 0 0;\">\n<li>Variational and message-passing inference</li>\n
