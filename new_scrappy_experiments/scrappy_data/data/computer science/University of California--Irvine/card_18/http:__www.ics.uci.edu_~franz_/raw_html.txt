He created an early just-in-time compilation system, contributed to the theory and practice of continuous compilation and optimization, and co-invented the trace compilation technology that eventually became the JavaScript engine in Mozilla's Firefox browser.</p>\n                  <p>His current\n                    research emphasis lies in the area of <strong>Software Systems</strong>, particularly focusing on compiler, virtual machine, and related system-level techniques for making software either <strong>safer</strong>, or <strong>faster</strong>, or <strong>both</strong>. Some of this research also falls under the labels <strong>Computer Security</strong>, <strong>Trustworthy Computing</strong>, and <strong>Software Engineering</strong>.</p>\n                  <p>Dr. Franz has graduated 28 Ph.D. students as their primary advisor and has published more than 140 peer-reviewed research papers. He is the\n                      Principal Investigator on many competitive grants from\n                      the federal government, totaling  over $19M (of which more than $11M as sole PI), and has received more than a million dollars in unrestricted gifts from industry in appreciation of the research innovations he has contributed.</p>\n                  <p>Franz received a Dr.\n                    sc. techn. degree in Computer Science (advisor: <a href=\"http://www.inf.ethz.ch/%7Ewirth\">Niklaus\n                      Wirth</a>) and a Dipl. Informatik-Ing. ETH degree, both\n                    from the <a href=\"http://www.ethz.ch/index_EN\">Swiss Federal Institute\n                      of Technology, ETH Zurich</a>.
