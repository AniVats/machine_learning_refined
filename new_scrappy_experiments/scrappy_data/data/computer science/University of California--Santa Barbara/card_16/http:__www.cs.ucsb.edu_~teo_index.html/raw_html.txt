He has been Professor of Computer Science at the\nOklahoma University (1975-1976)), Penn State University (1976-1979), ITESM (1979-1980),\nUniversity of Texas at Dallas (1980-1984) and the Univesity of California Santa Barbara (1984-2016).\nOn July 1st 2016 he became Professor Emeritus of Computer Science at the University of\nCalifornia, Sanat Barbara.\nIn 2009 Professor Gonzalez became IASTED Fellow for his \"contributions\nto Multicasting Dissemination Algotithms for Parallel and Distributed\nComputing, as well as for his decade long commitment to PDCS and IASTED\".\nProfessor Gonzalez research activity has concentrated\non the development of efficient\n<a href=\"publications/EXACT.html\">exact</a>\nand\n<a href=\"publications/APPROX.html\">approximation</a>\nalgorithms as well as\n<a href=\"publications/NPC.html\">computational complexity issues</a>\nfor problems in several disciplines.\n\n</p><p>\n<a href=\"temp.pdf\"> Current CV.</a>\n<a href=\"contribpre.html\"> most significant PREVIOUS main research contributions,</a>\n<a href=\"contribrec.html\"> most significant RECENT research contributions,</a>\nand \n<a href=\"contribother.html\"> other electronic journal publications (some\npublications).</a>\n\n</p><p>\nProfessor Gonzalez is currently working on\n<a href=\"publications/MM.html\">Multi-Message Multicasting</a>\nalgorithms for networks.  The is a fundamental problem that\narises when executing program in parallel computer systems.\nSome applications include iterative methods for solving systems\nof linear and non-linear equations, and most dynamic programming\nprocedures, etc.  His research contributions include work in\n<a href=\"publications/ROUT.html\">message routing</a>\nand\n<a href=\"publications/PDCS.html\">parallel and distributed computing.</a>\nHe has also developed efficient approximation algorithms for message dissemination problems\nin hypercubes and communication networks when communication links or nodes fail.\n\n\n<br>\n<img src=\"TGTC.JPG\" alt=\"PICTUREX\" align=\"left\" width=\"90\"> <br>\n</p><p>\nHe has also developed efficient approximation algorithms for\n<a href=\"publications/SCHED.html\">deterministic\nscheduling</a> problems.  These problems have applications\nin manufacturing systems as well as in task assignment in\nreal-time multiprocessor systems.\nHe has developed efficient algorithms for preemptive and\nnonpreemptive scheduling problems in open,\nflow and job shops, as\nwell as in identical, uniform and unrelated processor systems and\ngrid systems.\n\n</p><p>\nDr. Gonzalez has also investigated the complexity of generating exact and\napproximate solutions to a set of\n<a href=\"publications/CG.html\">computational geometry</a>\nproblems.  Specifically, he has\ndeveloped some very interesting algorithms for partitioning, grouping\nand covering problems.  These are robust algorithms that\ngeneralize to any number of dimensions.\nThese problems have applications in\ncomputer-aided design, cluster analysis, etc.\nRecently he has developed algorithms for corridor and\nbridge problems.\n\n\n<br>\n<img src=\"TGWP.JPG\" alt=\"PICTUREX\" align=\"left\" width=\"90\"> <br>\n</p><p> \nIn the area of\n<a href=\"publications/VLSIX.html\">computer-aided design of integrated circuits and systems</a>,\nhe developed efficient algorithms for component placement and\nwire routing for VLSI and MPCB systems.  Specifically, he has developed\nefficient algorithms for channel, rectangle, and switchbox routing problems, via\nassignment, via placement, pin redistribution, and layer assignment for VLSI and MPCB systems.\n\n\n</p><p>\nDr. Gonzalez has also developed\nexact and approximation algorithms\nfor\n<a href=\"publications/GRAPH.html\">graph problems,</a>\ncode minimization,
