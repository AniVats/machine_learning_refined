Almeroth is currently a\nProfessor in the \n<a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu/\">Department of Computer Science</a> \nat the \n<a href=\"http://www.ucsb.edu/\">University of California in Santa Barbara</a>\nwhere his main research interests include computer networks and protocols, \nwireless networking, multicast communication, large-scale \nmultimedia systems, and mobile applications.  At UCSB, \nDr. Almeroth was the founding Associate Director of the \n<a href=\"http://www.cits.ucsb.edu/\"> Center for Information Technology \nand Society (CITS)</a>, a founding faculty member of the \n<a href=\"http://www.mat.ucsb.edu/\">Media Arts and Technology (MAT) Program</a>,\n<a href=\"http://www.tmp.ucsb.edu/\">Technology Management Program (TMP)</a>,\nand the <a href=\"http://www.ce.ucsb.edu/\">Computer Engineering (CE) Program</a>.\nIn the research community, Dr. Almeroth has authored nearly 200 refereed \npapers. \n\nHe is the Associate Editor-in-Chief of IEEE Transactions on Mobile\nComputing (TMC) and on the Editorial Boards of several other journals\nand magazines; a member of the steering committees for the IEEE International\nConference on Network Protocols (ICNP), ACM Workshop on Challenged Networks\n(CHANTS), and the ACM Network and System Support for Digital Audio and \nVideo (NOSSDAV) workshop; and been actively involved in the organizing\ncommittees of numerous conferences and workshops.  Dr. Almeroth is the \nformer chair of the \n<a href=\"http://www.internet2.edu/multicast/\">Internet2 Working \nGroup on Multicast</a>, and is active\nin several working groups of the \n<a href=\"http://www.ietf.org/\">Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF)</a>.\nHe also serves on the boards of directors and/or advisory boards of \nseveral startups.  Dr. Almeroth has also served\nas an expert witness in a number of interesting patent cases.  He is a \nMember of the ACM and a Fellow of the IEEE.\n\n\n<br><br>\n<h4><li>Early Biography</li></h4>\n\nKevin Almeroth received his B.S. (1992), M.S. (1994), and Ph.D. (1997)\ndegrees in Computer Science from the <a href=\"http://www.gatech.edu/\"> \nGeorgia Institute of Technology</a>.  While at Georgia Tech, Kevin was\nadvised by <a href=\"http://www.cc.gatech.edu/fac/Mostafa.Ammar/index.html\">\nMostafa H. Ammar</a> who is a member of the \n<a href=\"http://www.cc.gatech.edu/computing/Telecomm/\">\nNetworking and Telecommunications Group</a> which is part of the\n<a href=\"http://www.cc.gatech.edu/\">\nCollege of Computing</a>.<p>\n\nKevin's dissertation title was\n\"<i>Networking and System Support for the Efficient, Scalable Delivery \nof Services in Interactive Multimedia Systems</i>\" and covered a range\nof topics including providing interactivity in video-on-demand (VoD)\nsystems; resource allocation in large-scale video delivery systems; a \nstudy of the dynamics of multicast group membership in the Multicast \nBackbone (MBone); and exploration of new paradigms for video programming\nover the Internet.\n</p></ul>\n\n\n<hr>\n<a name=\"research\"><h2>Research Projects and Publications</h2>\n\nAll of the research I'm involved in is summarized on the \n</a><a href=\"http://www.nmsl.cs.ucsb.edu/\">Networking and Multimedia Systems \nLab (NMSL)</a> WWW page.
