Engineering from\nthe University of California Santa Barbara in 1988. His research\ninterests are in cryptographic engineering, finite field arithmetic,\nrandom number generators, cyber-physical security, and \nneuromorphic engineering.\n<br><br>\n\nKo\u00e7 was an Assistant Professor at the University of Houston \n(1988-1992), Assistant, Associate and Full Professor at Oregon State\nUniversity (1992-2007). He established Information Security\nLaboratory at Oregon State University, and received Award for\nOutstanding and Sustained Research Leadership in September 2001.\n<br><br>\n\nCurrently, Ko\u00e7 has appointments at \u0130stinye University\n(\u0130stanbul, Turkey), Nanjing University of Aeronautics and\nAstronautics (Nanjing, China), and University of California Santa Barbara.\nHis research is funded by several private and national research\norganizations, performed within\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://koclab.org\">Ko\u00e7 Lab</a>\ncomposed of postdoctoral researchers, PhD and MS candidates,\nand undergraduate students.\n<br><br>\n\nKo\u00e7 is the co-founder of the\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://ches.iacr.org\">Conference on\nCryptographic Hardware and Embedded Systems</a> which\nthe second largest cryptography conference in the world.\nKo\u00e7 is also co-founder of\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://waifi.org\">International Workshop\non the Arithmetic of Finite Fields</a> (WAIFI) and\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://proofs-workshop.org\">Security\nProofs for Embedded Systems</a> (PROOFS), that\ncover efficient software and hardware realizations of finite fields\nand\nmethodologies that increase the confidence level in the security of\nembedded systems, respectively.\nKo\u00e7 also organized and chaired <i>Open Problems in\nMathematical and Computational Sciences Conference</i>,\nheld in September 18-20, 2013 in Istanbul, Turkey and\n<i>Cyber-Physical Security Education Workshop</i>,\nheld in July 17-19, 2017 in Paris, France.\n<br><br>\n\nKo\u00e7 was in the editorial boards of\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.computer.org/csdl/journal/tc\">IEEE\nTransactions on Computers</a> (2003-2008 and 2015-2019) and\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.computer.org/csdl/journal/tm\">IEEE\nTransactions on Mobile Computing</a> (2003-2007).\nHe was a guest co-editor of\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.computer.org/csdl/journal/tc/2003/04\">April\n2003</a>,\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.computer.org/csdl/journal/tc/2008/11\">November\n2008</a>, and\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://www.computer.org/csdl/journal/tc/2018/11\">November\n2018</a> issues of the IEEE Transactions on Computers\non cryptographic hardware and embedded systems,\nspecial-purpose hardware for cryptography and cryptanalysis,\nand cryptographic engineering in a post-quantum world.\nFurthermore, Ko\u00e7 is in the editorial boards of\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://www.worldscientific.com/page/ijfcs/editorial-board\">\nInternational Journal of Foundations of Computer Science</a>\nsince March 2016, and\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://tnuaa.nuaa.edu.cn/njhkhten/site/menu/20101109144206001\">\nTransactions of Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics</a>\nsince June 2019.\n<br><br>\n\nKo\u00e7 has designed, developed or made contributions to more\nthan 50 cryptographic software and hardware systems. Among them\nare PGP (Pretty Good Privacy), RSAREF and BSAFE (RSA Security),\nCDSA (Intel), TOR (The Onion Routing) and tens of embedded\nsoftware and hardware IP cores. According to an estimation made in\n2003, there were more than 4.5 billion copies of BSAFE running on\ndevices and computers. The IP cores containing algorithms and\narchitectures designed by Ko\u00e7 are found in products made\nby several companies including Texas Instruments, Intel, Samsung\nand Huawei. These algorithms and architectures are subjects of\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://cetinkoc.net/patents.html\">13</a> \nUS patents Ko\u00e7 co-authored.\n<br><br>\n\nKo\u00e7 is also the co-author of 4 books:\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://link.springer.com/book/10.1007%2F978-0-387-36682-1\">\nCryptographic Algorithms on Reconfigurable Hardware</a> (2007),\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://link.springer.com/book/10.1007%2F978-0-387-71817-0\">\nCryptographic Engineering</a> (2009),\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007%2F978-3-319-10683-0\">\nOpen Problems in Mathematics and Computational Science</a> (2014),\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-319-98935-8\">\nCyber-Physical Systems Security</a> (2018),\nall published by Springer.\nAmong them, <i>Cryptographic Engineering</i> is currently being\ntranslated to Chinese.\nAdditionally, a new book <i>Partially Homomorphic Cryptography</i>\nwill be published by Springer in 2020.\n<br><br>\n\nIn addition to contributing to\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://cetinkoc.net/proceedings.html\">7</a>\nconference proceedings as co-editor,\nKo\u00e7 has authored\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://cetinkoc.net/journal.html\">85</a>\njournal,\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://cetinkoc.net/conference.html\">57</a>\nconference,\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://cetinkoc.net/chapters.html\">11</a>\nbook chapters, and\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://cetinkoc.net/reports.html\">13</a>\ntechnical reports.\nKo\u00e7 supervised\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://cetinkoc.net/phd.html\">21</a> PhD students and\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://cetinkoc.net/grad.html\">40</a> MS students, and\ndirected research theses of\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"http://cetinkoc.net/ugrad.html\">6</a> undergraduate students.\nHe is among the top 3 scientists that supervised most\ndoctoral theses in cryptography.\nHis Google Scholar h-index is\n<a target=\"_blank\" href=\"https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&amp;user=pd-IjLcAAAAJ&amp;view_op=list_works&amp;pagesize=100\">44</a>.\nKo\u00e7 is among the top\n<a
