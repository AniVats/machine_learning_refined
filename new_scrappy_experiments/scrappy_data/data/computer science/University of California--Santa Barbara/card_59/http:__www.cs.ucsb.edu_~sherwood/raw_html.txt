Research<br>5163 Harold Frank Hall<br>sherwood@cs.ucsb.edu</h2><p class=\"\" style=\"white-space:pre-wrap;\">Hi!&nbsp; I am a Professor in the <a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu/\">Department of Computer Science</a>&nbsp;at <a href=\"http://www.ucsb.edu/\">UC Santa Barbara</a>, a member of the <a href=\"http://www.ce.ucsb.edu/\">Computer Engineering Program</a>, and hold a courtesy appointment in Electrical and Computer Engineering department.&nbsp; In addition to my responsibilities in <a href=\"https://engineering.ucsb.edu/\">Engineering</a>&nbsp;(which includes running many ongoing funded research projects through <a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu/~arch\">t</a>he <a href=\"/home\"><strong>ArchLab</strong></a>), I have served since 2016 as the UCSB <a href=\"https://www.research.ucsb.edu/about\">Associate Vice Chancellor for Research</a>&nbsp;and since 2014 as a co-founder to <a href=\"http://www.tortugalogic.com/\">Tortuga Logic</a>.&nbsp; Before joining UCSB in 2003, I received my B.S from UC Davis (1998) and my M.S. and Ph.D. from UC San Diego (2003) working with Professor <a href=\"https://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~calder/\">Brad Calder</a>.</p><p class=\"\" style=\"white-space:pre-wrap;\">UC Santa Barbara is an amazing place to work and live.&nbsp; I have been fortunate enough to find amazing students and collaborators here over the years eager to explore new ideas.&nbsp; Together we have done work engineering computers for places where <a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu/~sherwood/pubs/MICRO-09-leases.pdf\">security</a> and <a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu/~sherwood/pubs/ASPLOS-17-zarf.pdf\">correctness</a> are absolutely critical; finding new ways for <a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu/~sherwood/pubs/SBIM-12-spark.pdf\">hand drawn sketches to be used as a mode of interaction</a> with computers; probing the ability for new technologies such as <a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu/~sherwood/pubs/JETCAS-12-plasmon.pdf\">plasmonics</a>&nbsp;and <a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu/~sherwood/pubs/TCAD-13-3dsec.pdf\">3D integration</a> to be used to build faster, lower energy, and more secure computing; and supporting new models of computation from <a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu/~sherwood/pubs/ICCAD-15-timing.pdf\">cryptography</a>, to<a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu/~sherwood/pubs/CICC-17-racelogic.pdf\"> bioinformatics</a>, to machine learning from algorithms, to system software, to hardware.&nbsp; I am driven by a desire to understand the nature of computation better and support the next generation with the faster, more energy efficient, and more trustworthy machines they deserve.&nbsp; I hope you will consider <a href=\"/join\"><strong>joining us</strong></a>!</p></div></div><div class=\"sqs-block horizontalrule-block sqs-block-horizontalrule\" data-block-type=\"47\" html-block sqs-block-html\" data-block-type=\"2\" id=\"block-978beab267dbd8b329f3\"><div class=\"sqs-block-content\"><h2 style=\"white-space:pre-wrap;\">Lab Philosophy</h2></div></div></div><div class=\"col sqs-col-10 span-10\"><div class=\"sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html\" data-block-type=\"2\" id=\"block-2d369f95bdca623c8431\"><div class=\"sqs-block-content\"><p class=\"\" style=\"white-space:pre-wrap;\">The <a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu\">UCSB CS </a>Computer Architecture Lab (<a href=\"http://www.cs.ucsb.edu/~arch\">ArchLab</a>) is where my students and I work on all manner of computer science and engineering problems from the perspective of how we better \"shape\"&nbsp;computers to address our needs (e.g. to be more secure or amenable to machine learning).&nbsp; Computer architecture is a great field where creative thinking and careful software engineering meet crazy new hardware, surprising new algorithms, and complex new challenges.&nbsp; I try to keep our lab to a reasonable size so Ph.D. students get significant time directly with me one-on-one.&nbsp; I also try to keep a great mix of people with backgrounds and interests ranging from physics, to algorithms, to neuroscience, to hardware design, to human computer interferences.&nbsp; I do this because surrounding yourself with a diverse and capable pool of peers is one of the best ways to really learn and because it is a lot of fun that way.&nbsp; While my students and I all bring a variety of perspectives and experiences to the lab,&nbsp;we are also united in a common goal of doing groundbreaking and thought provoking architecture work that will help lay a foundation for the future of computing.&nbsp; If you are a perspective student or researcher I encourage you to consider <a href=\"/join\">joining us</a>.</p></div></div></div></div><div class=\"row sqs-row\"><div class=\"col sqs-col-12 span-12\"><div class=\"sqs-block horizontalrule-block Computer architecture is driven from the software side by user needs in terms of functionality and performance and from the hardware side by technological innovations and constraints. CS 154 introduces students to this exciting field, including the design process, performance and cost analysis, computer arithmetic, controller and data path design, input/output systems, interrupts and exceptions, pipelining and parallelism.</p><p class=\"\" style=\"white-space:pre-wrap;\"><em>CS254:&nbsp;Advanced Computer Architecture</em><br>This class will cover many of the important aspects of modern computer architectures (how do modern caches really work, predicting future execution, techniques for speculation and instruction level parallelism) and will explore the relationship between computer architecture and other areas such as machine learning and optimization.</p><p class=\"\" style=\"white-space:pre-wrap;\"><em>CS189B: Capstone</em><br>The capstone course sequence presents an opportunity to develop innovative solutions to real industry problems. You won't find answers to these problems in a textbook, on a message board, or in off-the-shelf software package -- because students in this class are among the first ever to be able to solve them. Working together with industry leaders, student teams take on the most challenging problems of the day with technological innovation, creativity, and boatloads of hard work.&nbsp; The capstone course
